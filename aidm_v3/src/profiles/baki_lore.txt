## Locations
The world of *Baki* is a "twin model" of our own, mirroring real-world geography, politics, and historical figures while integrating a secret, hyper-violent underground fighting culture. The setting is defined by legendary arenas, high-security prisons, and remote training grounds.

### **1. Primary Fighting Arenas**
*   **The Underground Arena (Kourakuen):**
    *   **Location:** Hidden in the **6th basement (B6)** of the **Tokyo Dome** in Tokyo, Japan.
    *   **Description:** Known as the "Holy Land of Fighting," it is a secret, no-holds-barred arena where the world's strongest martial artists compete. It is owned and operated by **Mitsunari Tokugawa**.
    *   **Rules:** There are four strict rules: No weapons, the arena takes no responsibility for injuries/death, no prize money is awarded, and champions are honored for eternity.
*   **The Raitai Tournament Venue:**
    *   **Location:** **China** (specifically associated with the Sea of Japan Raitai Tournament).
    *   **Description:** A massive arena used for the "Great Raitai Tournament," a competition held once every 100 years to determine the strongest "Kaioh" (martial arts master) in China.

### **2. Key Prisons and Facilities**
*   **Arizona State Prison (The Black Pentagon):**
    *   **Location:** **Arizona, USA**.
    *   **Description:** A high-security prison complex designed for criminals conventional law enforcement cannot handle. It is famously shaped like a pentagon and is the residence of **Biscuit Oliva** (the "Unchained").
    *   **Unique Setting:** Oliva lives in a 5-star luxury suite within the prison, serving as both an inmate and a "jailer" who captures other escaped convicts.
*   **The Colorado Saline Rock Site:**
    *   **Location:** **Colorado, USA**.
    *   **Description:** The site where the prehistoric man **Pickle** was discovered perfectly preserved in a saline rock formation alongside a Tyrannosaurus Rex.

### **3. Training Grounds and Residences**
*   **The Ida Mountains / Yasha Crag:**
    *   **Location:** A remote mountainous region in Japan, far from Tokyo.
    *   **Description:** This is where a 13-year-old Baki underwent extreme survival training. It is the habitat of the **Yasha Ape**, a monstrous primate that served as Baki’s first legendary opponent.
*   **Baki’s House:**
    *   **Location:** A small, graffiti-covered house in **Tokyo**.
    *   **Description:** Despite being the son of a billionaire (Emi Akezawa), Baki lives in a modest, run-down home. The walls are covered in "challenges" from thugs who frequently visit to test their strength against him, effectively turning his home into a 24/7 training gym.
*   **The Tokugawa Estate:**
    *   **Location:** A massive, traditional Japanese estate in **Tokyo**.
    *   **Description:** The residence of Mitsunari Tokugawa. It serves as a meeting hub for the series' elite fighters and was the primary residence for **Musashi Miyamoto** following his scientific revival.
*   **Shinshinkai Headquarters:**
    *   **Location:** Tokyo.
    *   **Description:** The main dojo for the Shinshinkai Karate organization, led by **Doppo Orochi**. It is one of the largest martial arts schools in the world, with over a million students.

### **4. World Geography and Political Setting**
*   **Real-World Parallels:** The series frequently features real-world locations such as **Shinjuku** (site of the final Father-Son battle), **Vietnam** (where Yujiro fought during the war), and the **United States** (specifically the White House).
*   **Political Geography:** The "Strongest Creature on Earth," Yujiro Hanma, is a geopolitical entity unto himself. He has signed non-aggression pacts with the United States, and the series features fictionalized versions of real US Presidents (e.g., George W. Bosh, Ozuma, and Tramp) who must personally swear an oath of peace to him.
*   **The "Floating Timeline":** While the series began in the early 1990s, the geography and technology evolve to match the modern era (introducing smartphones, cloning, and advanced cryogenics) while the characters age very slowly.

## Characters
The *Baki* series (including *Baki the Grappler*, *Baki*, *Hanma Baki*, and *Baki-Dou*) centers on the pursuit of ultimate strength. The story follows **Baki Hanma** as he trains to defeat his father, **Yujiro Hanma**, the strongest creature on Earth.

### **Protagonist: Baki Hanma**
*   **Role:** The titular protagonist and champion of the Underground Arena in Tokyo.
*   **Abilities:** 
    *   **Mixed Martial Arts:** Baki does not stick to one style; he absorbs techniques from every opponent he faces.
    *   **Shadowboxing/Visualization:** He can create mental projections of opponents (even giant insects) so vivid they cause physical damage to his body during training.
    *   **The Demon Back:** A genetic trait of the Hanma bloodline where the back muscles flex into the shape of a demon's face, exponentially increasing strength and durability.
*   **Relationships:** He is the son of Yujiro and Emi Akezawa. He has a romantic relationship with **Kozue Matsumoto**, who serves as his emotional anchor. He views his fellow arena fighters with deep respect.

### **Main Antagonist: Yujiro Hanma ("The Ogre")**
*   **Role:** The overarching antagonist and the "Final Boss" of the series. He is a force of nature who has defeated the entire U.S. military single-handedly.
*   **Abilities:**

    *   **Superhuman Physicality:** He has stopped earthquakes with a punch and can move faster than the human eye can track.
    *   **The Demon Back:** His back is permanently in this state when he fights seriously.
*   **Relationships:** He is the father of Baki and Jack. He murdered Baki’s mother, Emi, to "forge" Baki into a worthy opponent, creating a relationship defined by hatred, obsession, and a twisted form of respect.

## Power System
The power system in *Baki* (specifically *Grappler Baki*) is a unique blend of hyper-realistic martial arts, extreme pseudo-science, and "low-fantasy" genetic and psychic abilities. It is often described by fans as "testosterone-fueled logic," where sheer willpower and biological anomalies allow characters to perform god-like feats.

### **1. Core Mechanics**
The power system is divided into three primary pillars: **Genetics**, **Psychology (Imagination)**, and **Pseudo-Science**.

*   **The Hanma Bloodline (Genetic):** The ultimate "cheat code" in the series. Members of the Hanma family (Yujiro, Baki, Yuichiro) possess a unique physiology that allows for near-infinite growth.
    *   **Demon Back:** A physical manifestation where the back muscles flex into the shape of a demon's face. It acts as a massive multiplier for strength, speed, and durability.
    *   **Demon Brain:** A more recent revelation specifically for Baki, where his brain's frontal lobe is shaped like a demon. It grants superhuman reaction speeds and the ability to process complex combat data instantly.
*   **Imagination Training (Psychic/Mental):** Characters, particularly Baki, use "Image Training" to spar with imaginary opponents.
    *   **Subjective Reality:** If a fighter's imagination is strong enough, the "illusion" becomes real. Baki can spar with a giant praying mantis or a version of his father so realistic that he sustains actual physical injuries and bleeds from the "imaginary" hits.
*   **Total Concentration & Endorphins:** Characters can manually trigger chemical releases. Baki can release massive amounts of endorphins (often by twisting his ear) to ignore pain and enter a "flow state" that pushes his body past its limits.

### **2. Key Abilities & Techniques**
*   **0.5-Second Unconsciousness:** A "rule" of the Baki universe stating there is a 0.5-second gap between the brain's intent to move and the body's actual movement. Top-tier fighters can "see" this gap and strike while the opponent is technically unconscious.
*   **Aiki & Shaori:** Techniques that manipulate force. *Aiki* (used by Gouki Shibukawa) uses the opponent's own weight against them, while *Shaori* (used by Kaku Kaioh) allows a fighter to become "liquid" to absorb any impact or "solid" to deliver a strike with the weight of their entire body.
*   **Goudou (The Way of Biting):** Jack Hanma’s specialized style involving titanium teeth and surgical enhancements to treat biting as a legitimate martial art.

### **3. Rules & Limitations**
While the series seems lawless, it follows strict internal logic:
*   **The Knowledge Gap:** You cannot "imagine" what you don't understand. Baki could not use imagination training against the prehistoric man Pickle until he physically fought him and understood his "essence."
*   **Biological Constraints:** Non-Hanma characters have "hard caps." For example, Doppo Orochi is a master of Karate, but his human body can only take so much damage compared to Yujiro, who can tank lightning.
*   **One-Time Revitalization:** The Demon Back can "heal" or revitalize a fighter mid-battle, but it is typically a one-time trigger per fight. Once the adrenaline and Demon Back fade, the exhaustion is often life-threatening.

### **4. Tiers of Power**
The hierarchy is generally stable, with Yujiro Hanma serving as the "ceiling" of the verse.

| Tier | Rank | Key Characters | Feats / Context |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0** | **The Ogre** | Yujiro Hanma | Can stop earthquakes with a punch; stronger than the entire US military. |
| **1** | **Transcendent** | Baki Hanma, Musashi Miyamoto | Can challenge Yujiro; possess "reality-warping" levels of skill/imagination. |
| **2** | **Apex Predators** | Pickle, Kaku Kaioh, Jack Hanma | Prehistoric strength or 100+ years of perfected technique. |
| **3** | **World Class** | Biscuit Oliva, Hanayama, Retsu Kaioh | Peak physical specimens or masters of their respective arts. |
| **4** | **Masters** | Doppo Orochi, Gouki Shibukawa | Highly skilled but limited by age or human physiology. |
| **5** | **Street/Jobber** | Death Row Inmates, Sumo Jobbers | Dangerous to average humans, but fodder for the main cast. |

### **5. Progression**
Characters in Baki do not "level up" through traditional means; they progress through **Extreme Adaptation**:
1.  **Murderous Training:** Characters perform feats that would kill a normal person (e.g., Baki jumping off a cliff to trigger his survival instincts).
2.  **Fighting "Up":** The only way to get significantly stronger is to fight someone who can kill you. This "near-death focus" forces the body to evolve.
3.  **Surgical/Chemical Enhancement:** Used primarily by Jack Hanma, who uses bone-lengthening surgeries and massive steroid cocktails to bypass his natural genetic limits.

## Combat
Combat in the *Baki* series is a unique blend of hyper-realistic martial arts theory and completely impossible, over-the-top spectacle. It is defined by a "hyper-masculine" philosophy where fighting is the ultimate form of communication, identity, and evolution.

### **1. Core Fighting Styles**
The series features a wide array of styles, but they are often categorized by how they push the limits of human biology.
*   **Total Fighting (Baki Hanma):** Baki’s personal style is a hybrid of every martial art he has encountered (Boxing, Muay Thai, Karate, Judo, and Wrestling). His "Total Fighting" focuses on using the "best move at the best time," often switching between striking and grappling seamlessly.
*   **The Hanma Bloodline (Demon Back):** The ultimate "power-up" in the series. When a Hanma (Baki, Yujiro, or Yuichiro) reaches a peak state of combat, their back muscles flex into the shape of a demon's face. This grants superhuman strength, speed, and durability, effectively acting as a "shonen transformation" rooted in anatomy.
*   **Specialized Disciplines:**
    *   **Shinshinkai Karate (Doppo & Katsumi Orochi):** Focuses on "one-hit kill" strikes and defensive stances like *Sanchin*.
    *   **Chinese Kempo (Retsu Kaioh):** Emphasizes 4,000 years of refined technique, using everything from "invisible" strikes to walking on water.
    *   **Raw Brawling (Hanayama Kaoru):** A style of "pure strength" where the fighter refuses to use techniques or dodge, relying on massive durability and grip strength (the *Vice Grip*) to crush bones.

### **2. Tactical & Biological Warfare**
Fights in *Baki* are often won through "pseudo-science" tactics that exploit the human body’s weaknesses.
*   **The 0.5-Second Rule:** A core tactical concept where a fighter strikes in the 0.5-second gap between the brain sending a signal and the body actually moving. If timed perfectly, the opponent is hit while they are "unconscious" and unable to defend.
*   **Anatomical Exploits:** Characters like Kosho Shinogi use "Cord-Cutting" to sever nerves and tendons with their fingers, while others use "Benda" (Whip Strike), which focuses on the skin's pain receptors to cause agonizing pain without needing deep muscle damage.
*   **Environmental Mastery:** Gaia, a mercenary, uses "Environmental Warfare," turning everything from sand to the opponent's own body against them (e.g., the "Tunnel" technique, where he enters and exits an opponent's body through... unconventional means).

### **3. Emotional Stakes & Philosophy**
Battles are rarely just about winning; they are about the **imposition of will**.
*   **Father vs. Son:** The central conflict between Baki and Yujiro is an emotional "coming-of-age" story. Their final battle ends not with a knockout, but with an **imaginary dinner**. Baki "wins" by forcing the world's strongest man to play along with a mental projection of a home-cooked meal, proving Baki's will is equal to his father's.
*   **Respect through Violence:** Most of Baki’s enemies become his closest friends. In this world, a brutal fight is considered the highest form of respect and intimacy.
*   **The Burden of the "Strongest":** Characters like Yujiro live in a state of constant boredom because no one can challenge them. The emotional weight of the series often stems from the search for a "worthy" opponent.

### **4. Flashy Spectacle & Feats**
The "spectacle" of *Baki* comes from its disregard for the laws of physics in favor of "coolness."
*   **The "Dress" Technique:** Yujiro grabs Baki by the ankles and swings him so fast he becomes a blur of human "nunchucks," creating a visual "dress" of wind and violence around Yujiro.
*   **Imaginary Training:** Baki shadowboxes against a giant, 100kg praying mantis that he has "summoned" through sheer mental focus. The mental projection is so strong that Baki actually takes physical damage from the "ghost" insect.
*   **Over-the-Top Feats:**
    *   **Yujiro** stopping an earthquake with a single punch to the ground.
    *   **Pickle** (a prehistoric man) tackling a T-Rex and surviving a 30-meter fall by landing on his feet.
    *   **Hanayama** surviving a point-blank explosion of bullets in his mouth and continuing to fight.

## Adaptations
The *Baki* franchise, created by Keisuke Itagaki, is a massive martial arts saga spanning over 30 years. Because the series has been adapted by different studios across several decades, the relationship between the manga and anime can be complex.

### **1. Structural Timeline: Manga vs. Anime**
The story is divided into distinct "parts" in the manga, each with its own title. The anime adaptations generally follow these parts but often change the chronological order or skip content.

| Manga Part | Volumes | Anime Adaptation | Key Differences |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Part 1: Grappler Baki** (1991–1999) | 1–42 | **1994 OVA** & **2001 Series** | The 2001 anime reordered the story to be chronological, whereas the manga starts with Baki at 17 and uses a massive flashback for his childhood. |
| **Part 2: Baki** (1999–2005) | 1–31 | **Netflix Baki** (2018–2020) | Generally a 1:1 adaptation, but moved the "Revenge Tokyo" epilogue chapters. |
| **Part 3: Baki Hanma** (2005–2012) | 1–37 | **Netflix Baki Hanma** (2021–2023) | Mostly faithful, but the final "Father vs. Son" arc was criticized for being slightly rushed. |
| **Part 4: Baki-Dou** (2014–2018) | 1–22 | *Announced (2026)* | Focuses on the resurrection of Miyamoto Musashi. |
| **Part 5: Bakidou** (2018–2023) | 1–17 | *Not yet adapted* | Focuses on Sumo wrestling and the legend of Nomi no Sukune. |
| **Part 6: Baki Rahen** (2023–Present) | Ongoing | *Not yet adapted* | The current ongoing series. |

---

### **2. Major Adaptation Changes & Differences**

#### **The 2001 Series (Group TAC) vs. Manga**
*   **Chronological Shift:** In the manga, the story begins with the **Underground Arena Arc** (Baki is 17). The anime (2001) chose to start with the **Childhood Saga** (Baki is 13) to tell a linear story.
*   **Skipped Content:** The 2001 anime skipped the **Shinshinkai Karate Tournament** arc entirely because it had been adapted in the 1994 OVA. It also removed many "jobber" fights where Baki or side characters fought minor opponents to show off their strength.
*   **Heavy Censorship:** Because it aired on broadcast TV, the 2001 series censored extreme gore and sexual content. 
    *   **The Death of Emi (Baki's Mother):** In the manga, Baki carries his mother's dead body through the streets in a state of shock. In the anime, this was changed to him carrying her wedding ring.
    *   **Violence:** Many of Yujiro’s more brutal acts (like his treatment of the Yasha Ape) were toned down.

#### **The Netflix Era (TMS Entertainment) vs. Manga**
*   **Pickle's Height:** In the manga, the prehistoric man Pickle is depicted as a literal giant, often cited as being around 8 feet tall. In the anime, his height was stated as 6'5", making him look much smaller compared to characters like Jack Hanma.
*   **The Reporter Incident:** A controversial scene involving Pickle and a female reporter was heavily censored and shortened in the anime compared to the explicit and longer sequence in the manga.
*   **Skipped Monologues:** The "Father vs. Son" arc in the manga is famous for its long, philosophical monologues about the nature of strength and family. The anime cut several of these to keep the pacing fast for a streaming audience.
*   **Art Style:** The Netflix anime attempts to mimic Itagaki’s modern, hyper-detailed, and "grotesque" muscle anatomy. However, early seasons (2018) were criticized for using stiff 3D CGI for background fighters, a practice largely abandoned in the *Baki Hanma* seasons.

---

### **3. Version Guide: Which to Watch/Read?**
*   **The "Complete" Experience:** If you want every detail, the manga is essential. Itagaki’s art is unique and often "un-animatable" due to the sheer amount of shading and distorted perspective used to convey power.
*   **The 1994 OVA:** Often considered the most "aesthetic" version, it captures the gritty 90s anime style and covers the Karate Tournament arc that the 2001 series missed.
*   **The Netflix Series:** This is the most accessible version and is remarkably faithful to the "New Grappler Baki" and "Son of Ogre" storylines, despite minor cuts. It is the only way to see the modern arcs (Prison, Pickle, and Father-Son) in motion.
*   **Crossover:** In 2024, Netflix released ***Baki Hanma vs. Kengan Ashura***, an original non-canon crossover movie that does not exist in the manga.

## Tone
The *Baki* series (primarily *Baki the Grappler* and its sequels by Keisuke Itagaki) is a unique cultural phenomenon in the world of manga and anime. It is defined by a jarring but fascinating blend of hyper-violence, philosophical depth, and "deadpan absurdity."

### **1. Genre: Martial Arts & Hyper-Masculine Drama**
At its core, *Baki* is a **martial arts battle shonen/seinen**. However, it transcends the typical "tournament" genre by incorporating elements of:
*   **Body Horror:** The anatomical exaggerations and grotesque injuries often border on horror.
*   **Sports Science Fiction:** It uses pseudo-scientific explanations to justify impossible physical feats.
*   **Psychological Drama:** Specifically focusing on the trauma of the Hanma bloodline.

### **2. Tone: Deadpan Absurdity**
The tone of *Baki* is best described as **"taking the ridiculous with absolute seriousness."** 
*   **Hyper-Realism vs. Fantasy:** The art style is incredibly detailed regarding musculature and anatomy, yet the characters perform feats that defy physics (e.g., stopping an earthquake with a punch). 
*   **The "Baki Logic":** The series presents bizarre concepts—like a character becoming exponentially stronger by losing their virginity or fighting a 100kg imaginary praying mantis—with the gravity of a historical documentary. This creates a tone that is simultaneously epic and surreal.

### **3. Themes: The Nature of Strength**
*   **Toxic vs. Positive Masculinity:** The series is a massive commentary on masculinity. **Yujiro Hanma** represents "toxic" masculinity—strength used for domination, cruelty, and ego. **Baki** represents a shift toward "positive" masculinity—strength used for self-improvement, protection, and eventually, the simple desire to be "just a little stronger" than his father.
*   **The Father-Son Conflict:** This is the narrative spine of the series. It explores the "Oedipal" drive to surpass the father, but in a world where the father is a literal force of nature.
*   **Technique vs. Brute Force:** Many arcs explore whether refined martial arts can ever truly defeat "raw" natural power (represented by characters like Pickle or Biscuit Oliva).

### **4. Dark Comedy: Unintentional and Intentional**
*Baki* is frequently categorized as a **dark comedy** by fans, though the humor often stems from its sheer over-the-top nature.
*   **Grotesque Irony:** The series finds "humor" in the extreme lengths fighters go to, such as a character continuing to fight while their face is literally falling off, or the bizarre, long-winded monologues by "bystanders" who witness these fights.
*   **Satire of Hyper-Masculinity:** By pushing "manliness" to its absolute limit, the series often parodies the very genre it inhabits. The "Yujiro vs. The US President" scenes, where every sitting president must sign a peace treaty with a single man, are peak examples of this satirical dark humor.

### **5. Optimism: The Journey of Self-Actualization**
Despite the blood and tragedy, *Baki* is surprisingly **optimistic**.
*   **The Spirit of Improvement:** The series is a "love letter" to the grind. Baki’s relentless training and his ability to find respect for his opponents (even those who tried to kill him) reflect a core belief in human potential.
*   **Camaraderie:** There is a deep, strange "warrior’s respect" among the cast. Even after brutal mutilations, characters often share a meal or a laugh, suggesting that the struggle of combat is a way of truly "connecting" with others.

### **6. Atmosphere & Mood**
*   **Atmosphere:** Gritty, sweaty, and high-pressure. The air in *Baki* feels "heavy" with testosterone. The art uses heavy lines and distorted perspectives to make every punch feel like a seismic event.
*   **Mood:** The mood fluctuates between **Intense/High-Octane** during fights and **Contemplative/Surreal** during the "pseudo-science" explanations. It often feels like a fever dream where the rules of the world are being rewritten by the strongest person in the room.

| **Atmosphere** | Hyper-masculine, gritty, and surreal. |
| **Key Theme** | The philosophical and physical definition of "The Strongest." |

## Sequels, Series Aliases
The **Baki** franchise, created by Keisuke Itagaki, is a long-running martial arts epic known for its extreme muscularity, brutal combat, and complex publication history. The series is divided into several mainline sequels and numerous "Gaiden" (side story) spinoffs.

### **1. Mainline Series (Sequels)**
The story follows Baki Hanma’s journey to surpass his father, Yujiro Hanma. Each series is a direct chronological sequel to the previous one.

| Series Order | Japanese Title | Romanized / English Title | Common Abbreviation | Key Arc / Theme |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Part 1** | グラップラー刃牙 | *Grappler Baki* | GB | Childhood & Underground Arena |
| **Part 2** | バキ | *Baki* (New Grappler Baki) | NGB | Death Row Convicts & Raitai |
| **Part 3** | 範馬刃牙 | *Hanma Baki* (Son of Ogre) | BSO / SOO | Prison, Pickle, & Final Father-Son Fight |
| **Part 4** | 刃牙道 | *Baki-Dou* (2014) | BD14 | Musashi Miyamoto (Clone) |
| **Part 5** | バキ道 | *Bakidou* (2018) | BD18 | Nomi no Sukune II (Ancient Sumo) |
| **Part 6** | 刃牙らへん | *Baki Rahen* (2023–) | Rahen | Jack Hanma & "The People Around Baki" |

**Note on "Baki-Dou" vs. "Bakidou":** 
The fourth and fifth series are both titled *Baki-Dou* (Way of Baki). Fans distinguish them by the year of publication or the script used: 
*   **Part 4 (2014):** Uses **Kanji** for "Baki" (刃牙道).
*   **Part 5 (2018):** Uses **Katakana** for "Baki" (バキ道).

### **2. Spinoffs (Gaiden) & Prequels**
Spinoffs usually focus on side characters and are generally considered **canon** unless they are parodies.

*   **Baki Gaiden: Scarface (疵面):** Focuses on the Yakuza leader **Kaoru Hanayama**. It takes place between Part 2 and Part 3.
*   **Baki Gaiden: Kizuzura (創面):** A **prequel** focusing on Hanayama’s high school life.
*   **Baki Gaiden: Kenjin (拳刃):** A prequel focusing on a young **Doppo Orochi**.
*   **Baki Gaiden: Gaia:** Focuses on the mercenary **Gaia** (Nomura) and his military exploits.
*   **Baki Gaiden: Retsu Kaiou wa Isekai Tensei...:** An **alternate timeline/Isekai** where Retsu Kaioh is reincarnated into a fantasy world after his death in Part 4.
*   **Baki Gaiden: Yuuenchi (Amusement Park):** Originally a novel series, it follows **Mumon Katsuragi** (Katsumi Orochi's brother) and features characters from Itagaki's other series, *Garouden*.
*   **Baki Gaiden: Gaia to Sikorsky:** A comedic spinoff about Gaia and the death row convict Sikorsky living together.
*   **Baki: Revenge Tokyo:** A set of five special chapters (epilogues) showing the status of the five death row convicts after Part 2.

---

### **3. Continuity & Timeline**
The Baki timeline is linear, with the exception of specific flashback arcs and the Isekai spinoff.

*   **Chronological Order:** *Grappler Baki* (Childhood) → *Grappler Baki* (Arena/Tournament) → *Baki* → *Hanma Baki* → *Baki-Dou* (2014) → *Bakidou* (2018) → *Baki Rahen*.
*   **Canon Status:**
    *   **Canon:** All mainline series and most *Gaiden* series (Scarface, Kenjin, Kizuzura).
    *   **Non-Canon / Alternate:** *Baki Hanma vs. Kengan Ashura* (Crossover Movie), *Baki Domoe* (Parody), and the *Retsu Isekai* (Alternate Universe).
*   **Relationship to Original:** Every sequel builds directly on the power scaling of the previous. For example, characters who were "top tier" in the original *Grappler Baki* (like Doppo or Shibukawa) often become "mid-tier" as the series introduces prehistoric humans (Pickle) or legendary clones (Musashi).

### **4. Series Aliases & Terminology**
*   **"The Ogre":** Refers to Yujiro Hanma, the primary antagonist and goal for Baki.
*   **"Son of Ogre":** The official English subtitle for the third manga series (*Hanma Baki*).
*   **"New Grappler Baki":** The common fan/official name for the second series (*Baki*).
*   **"Baki-Dou":** Often translated as "The Way of Baki" or "Baki's Path."
*   **"Rahen":** Roughly translates to "around" or "vicinity," implying the story is shifting focus to the fighters surrounding Baki (specifically Jack Hanma).

## Factions
The world of *Baki* (Baki the Grappler) is defined by a hyper-masculine hierarchy where individual strength often supersedes national sovereignty. The series features a complex web of martial arts organizations, shadow government treaties, and ancient bloodlines.

### **1. Major Organizations & Factions**
The "Bakiverse" is built around several key pillars of power that organize the world's strongest fighters.

*   **The Underground Arena (Tokuen Arena):**
    *   **Leader:** Mitsunari Tokugawa (an eccentric billionaire).
    *   **Role:** Located six floors beneath the Tokyo Dome, this is the world’s most prestigious "no-rules" fighting arena. It operates outside the law, and its champion (currently Baki Hanma) is considered the strongest active fighter in the martial arts community.
*   **Shinshinkai Karate:**
    *   **Founder:** Doppo Orochi (the "God of War").
    *   **Scale:** With over one million students and thousands of dojos worldwide, it is the largest karate organization in the series. It serves as a primary source of "honorable" martial artists who often defend Japan against external threats.
*   **The Kaioh (Chinese Martial Arts Association):**
    *   **Leader:** Kaku Kaioh (Supreme Grandmaster, 146 years old).
    *   **Role:** "Kaioh" is a title granted to the masters of Chinese Kenpo. Every 100 years, they hold the **Raitai Tournament** to determine the "Kaioh of Kaiohs." They represent 4,000 years of martial arts history and view themselves as the pinnacle of technique.
*   **The Hanayama Group (Hanayama-gumi):**
    *   **Leader:** Kaoru Hanayama.
    *   **Role:** A powerful Yakuza syndicate. Unlike other criminal groups, they are led by a man who refuses to use weapons or martial arts, relying solely on "pure" brawling and a strict code of honor.

### **2. World Powers & Political Entities**
In *Baki*, world leaders are often depicted as being at the mercy of Yujiro Hanma, the "Strongest Creature on Earth."

*   **The United States of America:**
    *   **The Treaty of Friendship:** Since the Vietnam War, every U.S. president (including cameos of Reagan, Bush, Obama, Trump, and Biden) has been forced to sign a personal peace treaty with Yujiro Hanma. The U.S. recognizes Yujiro as a "one-man global superpower" and agrees not to interfere with his actions.
    *   **The Black Pentagon (Arizona State Prison):** A star-shaped prison designed to hold super-criminals. It is the residence of **Biscuit Oliva** (the "Unchained"), who acts as both a prisoner and a bounty hunter for the U.S. government.
*   **The JGSDF (Japan Ground Self-Defense Force):**
    *   **The Gaia Unit:** An elite special forces unit led by **Gaia** (Nomura). They specialize in "environmental combat" and are the only military force in Japan capable of even slightly challenging high-tier martial artists.
*   **The Republic of Metaman (Jun Guevaru’s Island):**
    *   A fictional sovereign nation founded by Jun Guevaru. It exists as a direct challenge to U.S. hegemony, protected solely by Guevaru’s individual strength.

### **3. The Hanma Bloodline**
The Hanma family is effectively its own faction, possessing a "Demon Back" (a muscular deformity that resembles a demon's face) which grants superhuman physical capabilities.
*   **Yuichiro Hanma:** The grandfather; famously defeated the U.S. Navy on an island during WWII.
*   **Yujiro Hanma:** The father; the ultimate antagonist and the "Ogre."
*   **Baki Hanma:** The protagonist; seeks to defeat his father to avenge his mother.
*   **Jack Hanma:** Baki’s half-brother; a "pit fighter" who uses extreme steroids and limb-lengthening surgery to gain power.

### **4. Major Alliances & Enemies**
*   **The Arena Defenders (Allies):** Baki, Doppo Orochi, Kaoru Hanayama, Goki Shibukawa, and Retsu Kaioh often form an informal alliance to protect the Underground Arena from "outsider" threats.
*   **The Five Most Evil Death Row Convicts (Enemies):** Five escaped prisoners (Spec, Dorian, Doyle, Sikorsky, and Yanagi) who traveled to Tokyo simultaneously to "know defeat." They represent "dirty" fighting, using poison, explosives, and deception.
*   **The Prehistoric Threat:** **Pickle**, a caveman preserved in saline who hunted T-Rexes. He is not "evil" but acts as a natural predator that the modern fighters must test themselves against.
*   **The Musashi Revival:** A faction created by Tokugawa using cloning and spiritualism to resurrect the legendary swordsman **Miyamoto Musashi**. Musashi became a public enemy after he began killing modern police and fighters with "invisible" and real swords.
*   **The Sumo Faction:** Led by **Nomi no Sukune II**, this group represents "Ancient Sumo," claiming that modern professional sumo is a diluted version of their original, bone-crushing combat style.

## Arcs
The *Baki* franchise, created by Keisuke Itagaki, is a long-running martial arts epic centered on **Baki Hanma’s** quest to surpass his father, **Yujiro Hanma**, the "Strongest Creature on Earth." The story is divided into several major manga series, each containing distinct sagas.

### **1. Grappler Baki (The Original Series)**
This series establishes Baki’s origins and his rise through the underground fighting world.

*   **Kid Saga (Age 13–15):** Baki undergoes brutal training to earn his father's respect. He fights 100 thugs, the Yakuza leader **Kaoru Hanayama**, and the monstrous **Yasha Ape**. The saga ends tragically when Yujiro kills Baki’s mother, **Emi Akezawa**, after she tries to protect Baki during their first duel.
*   **Underground Arena Saga (Age 17):** Three years later, Baki is the champion of the Tokyo Dome’s secret underground arena. He defends his title against masters like the "Cord-Cutter" **Shinogi Kosho** and the pro-wrestler **Mount Toba**.
*   **Maximum Tournament Saga:** Mitsunari Tokugawa organizes a 36-man tournament to find the world's strongest. Baki faces his half-brother, **Jack Hanma** (then Jack Hammer), in the finals. Baki wins, cementing his status as the world's top grappler.

### **2. Baki (New Grappler Baki)**
The stakes escalate as Baki faces international threats and legendary masters.

*   **Most Evil Death Row Convicts Saga:** Five death row inmates from around the world escape simultaneously and head to Tokyo to "know defeat." Baki and four other underground fighters (Doppo, Shibukawa, Hanayama, and Retsu) engage in a "no-rules" street war against them.
*   **Great Chinese Challenge (Raitai Tournament):** Baki is poisoned during the previous arc and travels to China to compete in the Centennial Raitai Tournament. He is cured mid-fight by "poison-on-poison" and helps the Japanese-American team defeat the Chinese Sea Kings.
*   **Godlike Clash of the Kids (Alai Jr. Arc):** Mohammad Alai Jr. challenges the underground fighters to prove his "New Alai Style" is superior. He eventually challenges Baki for both his title and his girlfriend, Kozue, but Baki defeats him instantly.

### **3. Baki Hanma (Son of Ogre)**
This series focuses entirely on the final preparations for the showdown with Yujiro.

*   **Combat Shadow Fighting Saga:** Baki hones his "image training," fighting a giant, 100kg mental projection of a praying mantis to push his limits.
*   **Great Prison Battle Saga:** Baki intentionally gets sent to the Arizona State Prison to fight **Biscuit Oliva**, the strongest man in the U.S. Baki defeats him in a pure test of strength, earning the title "Unchained."
*   **Pickle Wars Saga:** A prehistoric man, **Pickle**, is found preserved in ice. He is a predator who hunted T-Rexes. The world's top fighters challenge him; Retsu loses a leg, and Katsumi loses an arm. Baki eventually fights Pickle, choosing to lose a technical match to test his raw strength against the caveman.
*   **Warrior’s Struggle Saga:** A transitional arc where Retsu enters professional boxing and Baki has a surreal "normal" dinner with Yujiro, which serves as the formal prelude to their war.
*   **Strongest Father and Child Quarrel Saga:** The ultimate climax. Baki and Yujiro fight through the streets of Tokyo. The battle ends with Yujiro "cooking" an imaginary meal for Baki, acknowledging Baki as his equal and the "Strongest Man on Earth," while Yujiro remains the "Strongest Creature."

### **4. Baki-Dou (The Way of Baki - Kanji Version)**
*   **Musashi Miyamoto Arc:** Scientists clone the legendary samurai **Musashi Miyamoto** and revive his soul. Musashi proves too lethal for the modern world, killing **Retsu Kaioh** in a duel. The arc ends when Tokugawa’s sister, a medium, sucks Musashi’s soul out of his body during a fight with Baki.

### **5. Bakidou (The Way of Baki - Katakana Version)**
*   **Nomi no Sukune II Arc:** The descendant of the founder of Sumo, **Nomi no Sukune II**, appears. He easily defeats Oliva, prompting a tournament between the Baki cast and professional Sumo wrestlers. **Jack Hanma** returns with a new "Goudou" (Way of Biting) style and defeats Sukune in the finale.

### **6. Baki Rahen (Current Series)**
*   **Jack Hanma’s Path:** The current ongoing series shifts focus to **Jack Hanma**, who seeks to challenge the entire cast (including Baki and Yujiro) to prove the worth of his titanium teeth and "Goudou" style. Recent events include his rematches with Shinogi Kosho and Pickle.
