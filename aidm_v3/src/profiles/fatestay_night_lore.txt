## Locations
The setting of *Fate/stay night* is **Fuyuki City**, a fictional coastal city in Japan. Its design is heavily inspired by the real-world cities of **Kobe** and **Akashi** in Hyogo Prefecture. The city is geographically and culturally divided into two distinct districts by the **Mion River**.

### **1. Geography and Districts**
*   **Miyama Town (The Old Town):** Located on the western side of the river, this district is characterized by traditional Japanese architecture, older residences, and a quieter, historical atmosphere. It is home to the three founding families of the Holy Grail War.
*   **Shinto (The New Town):** Located on the eastern side, Shinto is a modern, bustling commercial district with skyscrapers, shopping malls, and a large park. It was the site of a massive fire 10 years prior to the story (the end of the 4th Holy Grail War), which led to its rapid redevelopment.
*   **Mion River & Fuyuki Bridge:** A wide river that bisects the city. The **Fuyuki Bridge** (based on the real-life **Kobe Bridge**) is a massive red steel structure connecting the two districts and serves as a frequent site for Servant battles.

---

### **2. Key Locations in Miyama Town**
*   **Emiya Residence:** Shirou Emiya’s home, a large Japanese-style estate. It serves as the primary base for the protagonist and Saber. Its real-world counterpart is the **Oda House Nagayamon** in Akashi.
*   **Tohsaka Mansion:** A Western-style mansion situated on a hill overlooking the town. It is the home of Rin Tohsaka and the headquarters of the Tohsaka family, who act as the "Second Owners" (managers) of Fuyuki’s spiritual land. Its exterior is based on the **Weathercock House** in Kobe.
*   **Matou Mansion:** A dark, foreboding Western-style estate where the Matou family resides. It contains the infamous "Worm Pit" in its basement. Its exterior is inspired by the **Uroko House** in Kobe.
*   **Homurahara Academy:** The high school attended by Shirou, Rin, and Sakura. It is built on a former military site. The school's archery hall is a direct replica of the one in **Akashi Park**.
*   **Ryudou Temple:** A Buddhist temple located atop **Mount Enzou** on the outskirts of Miyama. It is a powerful spiritual "leyline" point and serves as the base for Caster. It is based on **Yugyoji Temple** in Fujisawa.

---

### **3. Key Locations in Shinto**
*   **Fuyuki Church:** Located on a hill in Shinto, it serves as the neutral ground for the Holy Grail War, overseen by Kirei Kotomine. It is based on the **Oura Church** in Nagasaki.
*   **Fuyuki Central Park:** A large public space in Shinto built over the ruins of the residential area destroyed in the Great Fire. It is a significant site for the ritual's conclusion.
*   **Copenhagen Bar:** A local pub owned by the father of Shirou’s friend, Issei Ryudou, where characters occasionally gather.

---

### **4. Outer Regions & Hidden Sites**
*   **Einzbern Castle:** A massive, Western-style castle hidden within a dense, magically-shrouded forest on the outskirts of Fuyuki. It was built by the Einzbern family specifically for the Holy Grail War. Its design is based on the **Chateau de Brissac** in France.
*   **The Greater Grail Cavern:** A massive underground cave located directly beneath Mount Enzou. It houses the **Greater Grail**, the magical heart of the Holy Grail War system.
*   **The Four Spiritual Gates:** There are four specific locations in Fuyuki where the Holy Grail can manifest due to the convergence of leylines:
    1.  **Mount Enzou (Ryudou Temple)** – The primary and most stable location.
    2.  **Tohsaka Mansion**
    3.  **Fuyuki Church**
    4.  **Fuyuki Central Park** (The burnt field)

## Combat
Combat in *Fate/stay night* is a complex blend of high-stakes strategy, mythological lore, and visceral spectacle. It is defined by the **Holy Grail War**, a ritual where seven "Masters" (magi) summon "Servants" (Heroic Spirits from history or legend) to fight to the death.

### 1. The Tactical Foundation: Information & Mana
Combat is rarely just a test of strength; it is an **information war**.
*   **True Name Discernment:** A Servant’s identity is their greatest secret. If an opponent knows a Servant is King Arthur or Achilles, they immediately know their weaknesses (e.g., a specific heel or a code of honor). Battles often involve "probing" to force an enemy to reveal their **Noble Phantasm** (NP), which usually gives away their identity.
*   **Mana Management:** Servants are spiritual beings that require mana from their Master to manifest and fight. A Master with low magical energy (like Shirou) severely limits their Servant’s power, while a Master like Illya can fuel a "tank" like Berserker indefinitely.
*   **Command Seals:** Each Master has three seals that act as "absolute orders." Tactically, these are used as **emergency boosters** to teleport a Servant, force a "sure-hit" attack, or provide a massive temporary mana surge to survive a fatal blow.

### 2. Fighting Styles & Class Mechanics
Each Servant belongs to a "Class" that dictates their base stats and combat style:
*   **Saber (The All-Rounder):** Artoria Pendragon uses **Invisible Air**, a wind barrier that hides her sword's length and identity, making her strikes impossible to read. Her style is disciplined and relies on "Instinct," a skill bordering on precognition.
*   **Lancer (The Glass Cannon):** Cu Chulainn specializes in high-speed thrusts. His Noble Phantasm, **Gáe Bolg**, is a tactical nightmare: it **reverses causality**, meaning the "effect" (the heart being pierced) happens before the "cause" (the spear being thrust). It is undodgeable unless the target has a high "Luck" stat to literally change fate.
*   **Archer (The Tactician):** Unlike others, Archer (EMIYA) is a "counter-guardian" who fights with **projection magecraft**. He dual-wields the twin blades **Kanshou and Bakuya**, using a boomerang-like "Triple-Linked Crane Wings" combo to trap opponents. His ultimate move is **Unlimited Blade Works**, a Reality Marble containing every weapon he has ever seen.
*   **Berserker (The Unstoppable Force):** Heracles is a literal wall. His NP, **God Hand**, grants him 12 lives and makes him immune to any attack that has already killed him once. He fights with "Mad Enhancement," sacrificing sanity for overwhelming physical power.

### 3. Flashy Spectacle: The "Ufotable" Effect
While the original Visual Novel focuses on internal monologues and technical descriptions, the modern anime adaptations (by studio **ufotable**) transformed *Fate* into a benchmark for visual spectacle:
*   **Particle Effects & Lighting:** Battles are characterized by "digital cinematography," using vibrant lighting and particle effects (sparks, mana bursts) to emphasize the power of Noble Phantasms.
*   **Dynamic Camera Work:** The *Heaven's Feel* movies, in particular, use 3D environments to allow the camera to "fly" alongside Servants, capturing the sheer speed of combat (e.g., the Lancer vs. True Assassin chase).
*   **Impact Frames:** To convey the "superhuman" weight of these heroes, the animation uses brief, high-contrast frames to show the moment of impact, making every clash feel world-shaking.

### 4. Emotional Stakes & Narrative Battles
Battles in *Fate* are often "clashes of ideals" rather than just physical fights:
*   **Shirou vs. Archer (Unlimited Blade Works):** This is the emotional peak of the series. It is not a fight to see who is stronger, but a philosophical battle between a man’s youthful idealism and his future self’s cynical regret. The "spectacle" of the infinite swords serves as a backdrop to their dialogue.
*   **Saber vs. Rider (Heaven's Feel):** This battle represents the tragic fall of a hero. Saber, once the symbol of hope, is corrupted into "Saber Alter," forcing her allies to fight her. The emotional weight comes from the betrayal of her own legend.
*   **Berserker’s Last Stand:** In every route, Berserker’s battles are defined by his tragic role as a protector. His fight against Gilgamesh is a spectacle of "unyielding will," where he continues to move even after losing all 12 lives, driven solely by his bond with his Master, Illya.

## Adaptations
*Fate/stay night* is a complex franchise because it originated as a branching visual novel (VN) with three distinct storylines (routes): **Fate**, **Unlimited Blade Works**, and **Heaven’s Feel**. Because each route focuses on a different heroine and theme, no single anime or manga adaptation covers the entire story perfectly.

### **1. The Three Main Routes & Their Themes**
| Route | Heroine | Theme | Primary Adaptation(s) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fate** | Saber | "Oneself as an ideal" | 2006 Anime (Studio Deen) |
| **Unlimited Blade Works** | Rin Tohsaka | "Struggling with oneself as an ideal" | 2014 TV Series (ufotable) |
| **Heaven’s Feel** | Sakura Matou | "The friction between real and ideal" | 2017–2020 Movie Trilogy (ufotable) |

---

### **2. Anime vs. Manga: Major Adaptations & Differences**

#### **A. The "Fate" Route (Saber's Story)**
*   **Anime (2006, Studio Deen):** This was the first adaptation. While it primarily follows the Fate route, it famously "mashes up" elements from the other two routes (UBW and Heaven's Feel) to add more action. 
    *   **Key Change:** In the anime, Caster captures Saber and holds her hostage, a plot point taken from the UBW route. In the original Fate route of the VN, this never happens.
*   **Manga (2006–2012, Datto Nishiwaki):** Like the Deen anime, this manga is a "hybrid" adaptation. It starts with the Fate route but incorporates significant portions of Unlimited Blade Works.
    *   **Difference:** It is generally considered a "rough" adaptation by fans. It lacks the psychological depth of the VN and, like the 2006 anime, creates a disjointed narrative by trying to cover too much at once.

#### **B. Unlimited Blade Works (Rin's Story)**
*   **Anime (2014, ufotable):** A highly faithful 26-episode series. It is praised for its "unlimited budget" visuals but criticized for cutting many of Shirou Emiya’s internal monologues.
    *   **Key Change:** Without his internal thoughts, Shirou can appear "bland" or "reckless" to anime-only viewers, whereas the VN explains his survivor's guilt and trauma in detail.
*   **Manga (2021–Present, Daisuke Moriyama):** A newer adaptation that is currently ongoing.
    *   **Difference:** Fans consider this manga superior to the anime in terms of characterization. It restores Shirou’s inner monologues and expressive facial features, making his psychological struggle against Archer much clearer.

#### **C. Heaven’s Feel (Sakura's Story)**
*   **Anime (2017–2020, ufotable Movie Trilogy):** Three high-production films (*Presage Flower*, *Lost Butterfly*, *Spring Song*). 
    *   **Key Change:** Due to the limited runtime of movies, significant character development for **Illya** and **Rider** was cut. The movies also skip the first three days of the war (assuming the viewer has already seen UBW) and use a montage instead.
*   **Manga (2015–Present, Taskohna):** This manga is widely considered the most faithful adaptation of the third route.
    *   **Difference:** It includes the "Day 1–3" events and the extensive dialogue between Shirou and Illya that the movies omitted. It is much slower-paced but provides a more complete narrative experience.

---

### **3. Summary of Key Version Differences**

| Feature | Visual Novel (Source) | Anime Adaptations | Manga Adaptations |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Perspective** | First-person (Shirou) | Third-person (Cinematic) | Third-person (Expressive) |
| **Internal Depth** | Extremely High (Monologues) | Low (Prioritizes action/visuals) | Moderate-High (Restores text) |
| **Route Integrity** | Strict (Choose one path) | Mixed (2006) or Strict (Ufo) | Mixed (2006) or Strict (Newer) |
| **Censorship** | Contains "H-scenes" (Original) | Completely removed | Mostly removed (Taskohna's HF is the most explicit) |

### **Which version should you choose?**
*   **For the "Complete" Story:** The **Visual Novel** (specifically the 2024 *Fate/stay night Remastered*) is the only way to see the full, unedited story.
*   **For Visuals/Action:** The **ufotable anime** (UBW series and HF movies) are industry-leading in terms of animation quality.
*   **For Character Depth:** The **newer manga** (Moriyama's UBW and Taskohna's HF) are better than the anime for understanding the characters' motivations, though they are still being released.

## Characters
*Fate/stay night* is a visual novel and anime franchise centered on the **Fifth Holy Grail War**, a secret battle royale where seven mages (Masters) summon legendary heroes (Servants) to fight for a wish-granting relic.

### **1. The Protagonist: Shirou Emiya**
*   **Role:** An idealistic high school student and survivor of the Fourth Holy Grail War's aftermath. He accidentally summons Saber and enters the war to protect others.
*   **Abilities:**
    *   **Reinforcement:** Enhancing the physical properties of objects (e.g., making a pipe harder than steel).
    *   **Projection (Tracing):** Materializing objects from his mind. Unlike standard projection, Shirou "traces" the entire history and composition of a weapon, allowing him to replicate its power and the original wielder’s skill.
    *   **Unlimited Blade Works:** His Reality Marble, a mental world containing every weapon he has ever seen.
    *   **Avalon:** A legendary scabbard implanted in him as a child that provides passive, near-instant healing when Saber is nearby.
*   **Relationships:** He is the adoptive son of **Kiritsugu Emiya** and the adoptive brother of **Illyasviel von Einzbern**. He serves as the romantic interest for the three main heroines depending on the story route.

---

### **2. The Three Heroines**
Each heroine represents one of the three narrative "routes" of the story.

#### **Saber (Artoria Pendragon)**
*   **Role:** Shirou’s Servant and the heroine of the *Fate* route. She is the "King of Knights" (King Arthur).
*   **Abilities:** Master swordsmanship, **Mana Burst** (using magical energy to boost physical power), and **Instinct** (near-precognition). Her Noble Phantasms include **Invisible Air** (hiding her sword) and **Excalibur** (a beam of holy light).
*   **Relationships:** Initially views Shirou as an incompetent Master but grows to love him. She has a traumatic history with his father, Kiritsugu.

#### **Rin Tohsaka**
*   **Role:** A prodigy magus and the heroine of the *Unlimited Blade Works* route. She is the Master of Archer.
*   **Abilities:** **Jewel Magecraft** (storing massive energy in gems to fire as spells) and **Gandr** (energy bullets). She is an "Average One," capable of manipulating all five elements.
*   **Relationships:** Shirou’s schoolmate and rival-turned-ally. She is the biological older sister of Sakura Matou, though they were separated as children.

#### **Sakura Matou**
*   **Role:** Shirou’s underclassman and the heroine of the *Heaven's Feel* route. She is the true Master of Rider.
*   **Abilities:** Initially appears powerless, but possesses **Imaginary Numbers** magic. In *Heaven's Feel*, she becomes the **Dark Grail**, capable of absorbing Servants and manipulating shadows that devour everything they touch.
*   **Relationships:** Deeply in love with Shirou. She suffers under the abuse of the Matou family, specifically her brother Shinji and grandfather Zouken.

---

### **3. Major Antagonists**
The "villain" role shifts significantly between the three routes.

*   **Kirei Kotomine:** The main antagonist of the franchise. He is a priest and the "Overseer" of the war who secretly manipulates the conflict for his own sadistic pleasure. He uses **Black Keys** (throwing daggers) and **Super Bajiquan** (martial arts).
*   **Gilgamesh:** The "King of Heroes" and the primary antagonist of the *Fate* and *UBW* routes. He possesses the **Gate of Babylon**, which contains the prototypes of every legendary treasure. His ultimate weapon is **Ea (Enuma Elish)**, a sword that can tear through reality.
*   **Archer (EMIYA):** A cynical Servant summoned by Rin. In the *UBW* route, he acts as an antagonist to Shirou. It is revealed that he is **Shirou Emiya from the future**, who became a "Counter Guardian" and now seeks to kill his past self to end his own eternal suffering.
*   **Zouken Matou:** The ancient patriarch of the Matou family and the primary antagonist of *Heaven's Feel*. He is a soul-consuming magus who exists as a swarm of **Crest Worms**.
*   **Shinji Matou:** Sakura’s brother and a minor antagonist. Arrogant and cruel, he acts as a "False Master" for Rider using a magical book.

## Arcs
*Fate/stay night* is a visual novel structured into three distinct story "routes" that branch from a common beginning. Each route focuses on a different heroine and explores a unique thematic perspective on the protagonist Shirou Emiya’s ideal of becoming a "Hero of Justice."

### **1. The Common Route (Days 1–3)**
The story begins with a **Prologue** told from the perspective of Rin Tohsaka, who summons the Servant Archer. The perspective then shifts to Shirou Emiya.
*   **Day 1–2:** Shirou lives a mundane life as a high school student and amateur magus. He is warned by a mysterious girl (Illyasviel) that he will die soon.
*   **Day 3 (The Branch Point):** Shirou witnesses a battle between Archer and Lancer at school. Lancer kills him to silence him, but Rin revives him using a jewel pendant. Lancer returns to finish the job at Shirou’s home, leading Shirou to accidentally summon **Saber**.
    *   **The Split:** Saber immediately attacks Rin and Archer.
        *   **Fate Route:** Shirou does *not* stop Saber; she wounds Archer, removing him from the early plot.
        *   **Unlimited Blade Works:** Shirou uses a Command Spell to stop Saber; Archer remains active.
        *   **Heaven’s Feel:** Accessed if Shirou has spent significant time with Sakura Matou in the preceding days.

---

### **2. Major Story Arcs & Plot Summaries**

#### **Route 1: Fate (The Saber Route)**
*   **Theme:** "Oneself as an Ideal."
*   **Heroine:** Saber (Artoria Pendragon).
*   **Summary:** Shirou and Rin form an alliance. The story focuses on the mystery of Saber’s identity and her tragic past as King Arthur. Shirou falls in love with her and opposes her wish to use the Holy Grail to rewrite her history, arguing that she must accept her life as it was.
*   **Major Events:**
    *   **Berserker Battle:** The first major hurdle where Shirou is nearly killed protecting Saber.
    *   **Caster Arc:** Caster kidnaps Shirou to lure Saber into a trap at Ryuudou Temple.
    *   **The Truth of the Grail:** Shirou learns that the Grail is corrupted and that his father, Kiritsugu, was the one who ordered its destruction 10 years ago.
    *   **Final Battle:** Shirou and Saber face Gilgamesh and Kirei Kotomine. Saber destroys the Grail and disappears, finally finding peace.
*   **Ending:** Saber returns to her time to die. In the *Réalta Nua* version, a "Last Episode" shows Shirou and Saber reuniting in Avalon.

#### **Route 2: Unlimited Blade Works (The Rin Route)**
*   **Theme:** "Struggling with oneself as an ideal."
*   **Heroine:** Rin Tohsaka.
*   **Summary:** This route focuses on the Servant **Archer**, who is revealed to be a future version of Shirou Emiya. Archer has become a "Counter Guardian" and is so disillusioned by his life of endless killing that he intends to kill his past self (Shirou) to create a paradox and erase his existence.
*   **Major Events:**
    *   **Caster’s Betrayal:** Caster steals Saber from Shirou using *Rule Breaker*.
    *   **Archer’s Defection:** Archer betrays Rin to join Caster, only to later kill Caster and her Master to pursue his own goal: killing Shirou.
    *   **Shirou vs. Archer:** A clash of ideals where Shirou acknowledges Archer’s logic but refuses to abandon his dream. Shirou manifests his own Reality Marble, *Unlimited Blade Works*.
    *   **Gilgamesh’s Plan:** Gilgamesh attempts to use Shinji Matou as a vessel for the corrupted Grail.
*   **Ending:** Shirou defeats Gilgamesh. In the **True End**, he and Rin go to London to study at the Mages' Association.

#### **Route 3: Heaven’s Feel (The Sakura Route)**
*   **Theme:** "The friction between the real and the ideal."
*   **Heroine:** Sakura Matou.
*   **Summary:** The darkest route, revealing the true nature of the Holy Grail War. A mysterious "Shadow" begins consuming Servants and citizens. Shirou discovers Sakura has been brutally abused by the Matou family and is the true vessel for the Grail's corruption. He must choose between his ideal of saving everyone or abandoning that ideal to save the woman he loves.
*   **Major Events:**
    *   **The Shadow’s Emergence:** The Shadow kills Lancer and consumes Saber, turning her into **Saber Alter**.
    *   **Archer’s Sacrifice:** Archer is mortally wounded; his arm is transplanted onto Shirou to save him, giving Shirou immense but self-destructive power.
    *   **Dark Sakura:** Sakura is consumed by the Grail’s "All the World's Evil" (Angra Mainyu) and becomes a villain.
    *   **The Final Duel:** Shirou fights Kirei Kotomine at the site of the Grail.
*   **Ending:** In the **True End**, Illya sacrifices herself to save Shirou, and he lives a quiet life with Sakura and Rider.

## Power System
The power system of *Fate/stay night* (and the broader Nasuverse) is a complex, multi-layered hierarchy that blends scientific theory with occult mysticism. It is governed by rigid laws that prioritize "Mystery" and "Equivalent Exchange."

### **1. Core Mechanics: The Energy Source**
The system operates on **Magical Energy** (often called Prana), which is processed through **Magic Circuits**.
*   **Magic Circuits:** Pseudo-nerves in the soul that convert life force into magical energy. They are innate; a magus is born with a set number (Quantity) and a specific efficiency (Quality). They cannot be "trained" to increase in number, making lineage and selective breeding vital for magi.
*   **Od vs. Mana:** 
    *   **Od:** Energy generated internally by a living being (the "Lesser Source").
    *   **Mana:** Energy existing in the environment/nature (the "Greater Source"). 
*   **Conversion:** Magi use their circuits to draw in Mana or use their own Od to fuel "Foundations"—pre-established systems of magic (like Runes or Alchemy).

### **2. Abilities: Magecraft vs. Magic**
The system makes a sharp distinction between what is scientifically possible and what is a "miracle."
*   **Magecraft (Thaumaturgy):** The artificial reenactment of phenomena. If a result can be achieved through science or time (e.g., creating fire with a lighter), doing it with prana is Magecraft. 
*   **True Magic (Sorcery):** Phenomena that are impossible to replicate with science or Magecraft, regardless of time or resources. There are only **Five True Magics** remaining (e.g., the Second Magic: Kaleidoscope, which allows travel between parallel worlds).
*   **Origin & Element:** 
    *   **Origin:** The "starting point" of a soul's existence (e.g., Shirou’s is "Sword"). It dictates a person's fundamental nature and instincts.
    *   **Element:** The "flavor" of a magus's energy (Fire, Water, Earth, Wind, Ether). A magus is usually limited to spells matching their element.

### **3. Rules and Limitations**
*   **The Principle of Mystery:** The power of an ability is tied to its "Mystery"—how secret and ancient it is. As science explains a phenomenon, its Mystery (and thus its magical power) fades. This is why modern magi are generally weaker than those from the "Age of Gods."
*   **Equivalent Exchange:** Magecraft cannot create something from nothing. It requires a cost, usually in the form of magical energy or physical materials.
*   **World Self-Correction (Gaia):** The World itself (Gaia) has a "will" to maintain its laws. If a magus tries to impose something unnatural (like a **Reality Marble**, an inner world manifested outwardly), the World will actively try to crush it, making such feats extremely draining and temporary.
*   **The Counter Force:** A safety mechanism of the World. **Gaia** protects the planet, while **Alaya** (the collective will of humanity) protects the human race. It summons "Counter Guardians" (like Archer) to eliminate threats to human survival.

### **4. Tiers of Power**
Power is often measured by the **Ranking System (E to A, and EX)**:
*   **E to A:** Numerical representations where E = 10 and A = 50. A "+" modifier (e.g., A+) means the value can momentarily double.
*   **EX:** "Immeasurable." This doesn't always mean "strongest"; it means the ability falls outside the standard scale (e.g., Gilgamesh’s *Ea*).

**General Hierarchy:**
1.  **Ultimate Ones (Types):** Manifestations of planets; virtually invincible.
2.  **Divine Spirits / True Ancestors:** Gods and high-tier vampires.
3.  **Servants / Heroic Spirits:** Legendary figures given physical form.
4.  **Dead Apostles:** High-tier vampires.
5.  **Magi:** Human practitioners. Even a "Grand" rank magus is usually inferior to an average Servant in direct combat.

### **5. Progression and Growth**
*   **Magi Progression:** 
    *   **Magic Crests:** The primary way magi "progress" across generations. A Crest is a database of a family's entire history of research, transplanted like an organ into the heir.
    *   **Clock Tower Ranks:** The Mage's Association ranks members from **Frame** (lowest) to **Grand** (highest).
    *   **The Root (Akasha):** The ultimate goal of all magi. Reaching the Root grants True Magic and "God-like" knowledge.
*   **Servant Progression:** 
    *   **Fame/Land Boost:** A Servant is stronger if summoned in the land where their legend originated or if they are highly famous there (e.g., Vlad III is a monster in Romania but average elsewhere).
    *   **Master Compatibility:** A Master with high-quality Magic Circuits can provide more prana, allowing a Servant to fight at their "legendary" peak. A poor Master (like Shirou initially) forces a Servant to fight with lowered stats.

## Sequels, Series Aliases
*Fate/stay night* (フェイト/ステイナイト, *Feito/Sutei Naito*) is the foundation of a massive multimedia franchise created by **Type-Moon**. Originally a 2004 visual novel (VN) written by **Kinoko Nasu**, it has expanded into a complex multiverse known as the **Nasuverse**.

### **1. The Original Series (The Core)**
The original visual novel is divided into three narrative "routes" that branch from the same starting point. Each route is considered equally canon within the multiverse.

*   **Fate (Saber Route):** Focuses on the relationship between Shirou and Saber. (Adapted by Studio Deen in 2006).
*   **Unlimited Blade Works (Rin Route):** Focuses on Shirou’s ideals and his conflict with Archer. (Adapted as a film by Deen in 2010 and a TV series by ufotable in 2014).
*   **Heaven’s Feel (Sakura Route):** A darker, deconstructive route focusing on the Matou family and the Grail's corruption. (Adapted as a film trilogy by ufotable, 2017–2020).

---

### **2. Sequels and Prequels**
*   **Fate/Zero (Prequel):** A light novel series by Gen Urobuchi. It depicts the **4th Holy Grail War** (10 years prior). While it is a prequel, it is technically an "alternate timeline" prequel because of minor lore contradictions with the original VN.
*   **Fate/hollow ataraxia (Sequel):** A visual novel set six months after the events of *Fate/stay night*. It uses a "time loop" mechanic to allow characters from all three routes to coexist, serving as both a fan-service-heavy slice-of-life and a mystery thriller.
*   **Lord El-Melloi II Case Files:** A series following Waver Velvet (from *Fate/Zero*) as a professor at the Clock Tower. It acts as a bridge between *Zero* and *Stay Night*.

---

Most spinoffs take place in alternate universes where a specific historical event diverged (usually during the 3rd Holy Grail War).

### **4. Continuity and "Canon"**
In the Nasuverse, "canon" is governed by the **Multiverse Theory**:
*   **Pruning Theoretical Phenomena:** The universe "culls" timelines that have stagnated or reached a dead end (Lostbelts).
*   **Greater History of Man (Pan-Human History):** The "main" timelines that are allowed to continue.
*   **Relationship:** Every *Fate* story is canon to its own timeline. Fans often distinguish between "Fate-worlds" (where Heroic Spirits can be summoned) and "Tsukihime-worlds" (where Human Order is weaker and Vampires are more prominent).

## Factions
The world of *Fate/stay night* is defined by a hidden supernatural society governed by ancient organizations and cosmic forces. The factions range from political mage bodies to the literal collective will of the planet.

### **1. Major Global Organizations**
The two primary "world powers" in the supernatural community exist in a state of "Cold War," maintaining a fragile non-aggression pact to keep the existence of magic secret from the mundane world.

*   **The Mage's Association (Clock Tower):**
    *   **Role:** The governing body for all magi, headquartered in London (the Clock Tower). It functions as a university, government, and research center.
    *   **Goal:** The pursuit of the "Root" (Akasha), the source of all knowledge and power.
    *   **Enforcement:** They employ **Enforcers** to hunt down "heretical" magi or those who threaten the secrecy of magecraft. They also issue **Sealing Designations**, which involve capturing a magus to preserve their unique magical lineage (often by turning them into a biological specimen).
*   **The Holy Church:**
    *   **Role:** A massive religious organization that views magecraft as heresy. However, they use their own form of magic called **Sacraments** (miracles powered by faith).
    *   **Goal:** To eliminate all supernatural threats to humanity and secure "Holy Grails" (artifacts that claim to be the cup of Christ).
    *   **Enforcement:** They use **Executors**—elite inquisitors like Kirei Kotomine—to hunt vampires and magi. The **Burial Agency** is their most elite, shadow-ops unit.

### **2. The Three Founding Families (The Gosanke)**
These three families created the Holy Grail War ritual in Fuyuki City roughly 200 years ago. Each provided a specific component for the system.

*   **The Einzbern Family:**
    *   **Specialty:** Alchemy and the creation of Homunculi.
    *   **Contribution:** They provided the **vessel** for the Holy Grail.
    *   **Status:** A reclusive family of homunculi in Germany. They are desperate to win to reclaim the "Third Magic" (Heaven's Feel), which they lost centuries ago.
*   **The Tohsaka Family:**
    *   **Specialty:** Jewel Magecraft and conversion of energy.
    *   **Contribution:** They provided the **spiritual land** (Fuyuki City) and the summoning rituals.
    *   **Status:** A prestigious lineage that maintains a balance between the Association and the Church. Rin Tohsaka is the current head.
*   **The Matou (formerly Makiri) Family:**
    *   **Specialty:** Familiars (specifically worms) and "binding" magecraft.
    *   **Contribution:** They created the **Command Seals** that allow Masters to control Servants.
    *   **Status:** A declining family whose magical circuits have withered. They are led by the ancient, soul-rotting patriarch **Zouken Matou**.

### **3. Cosmological World Powers**
The "World" itself acts as a faction through two distinct defensive mechanisms known as the **Counter Force**.

*   **Alaya (The Will of Humanity):** The collective unconsciousness of mankind. It seeks to prevent the extinction of the human race. It summons **Counter Guardians** (like Archer/EMIYA) to eliminate threats to humanity.
*   **Gaia (The Will of the Planet):** The spirit of the Earth itself. It seeks to protect the planet, sometimes even from humanity if they become too destructive. It empowers nature spirits and "True Ancestors."

### **4. Fifth Holy Grail War Alliances & Enemies**
The war is a battle royale between seven Master-Servant pairs, but alliances shift depending on the story route:

*   **The Protagonist Alliance:** Shirou Emiya (Saber) and Rin Tohsaka (Archer) frequently form a truce to survive more powerful threats.
*   **The Temple Faction:** Caster (Medea) and her Master, Souichirou Kuzuki, occupy Ryuudou Temple. They act as a major early-game enemy, having illegally summoned their own Servant, Assassin.
*   **The Church Supervisor:** **Kirei Kotomine** is officially the neutral mediator. In reality, he is a primary antagonist who manipulates the war for his own amusement, secretly commanding **Gilgamesh** (the "eighth" Servant left over from the previous war).
*   **The Shadow/Zouken (Heaven's Feel Route):** In the final route, the Matou patriarch Zouken emerges as the "True Enemy," utilizing a corrupting "Shadow" and a blackened version of the Grail to consume other participants.

### **5. The Mundane World**
While not an active "faction," the mundane human world exerts power through the **Decline of Mystery**. In this universe, as science explains the world, magic (Mystery) becomes weaker. This forces all magical organizations to operate in total secrecy; if the public learns how a spell works, that spell loses its power.

## Tone
*Fate/stay night* is a seminal Japanese visual novel and media franchise known for its complex narrative structure and deep philosophical underpinnings. Its tone and themes evolve significantly across its three main story routes—**Fate**, **Unlimited Blade Works**, and **Heaven’s Feel**—moving from a traditional heroic fantasy to a deconstructive psychological thriller.

### **1. Tone: The Balance of Grimness and Levity**
The overall tone of *Fate/stay night* is **serious and high-stakes**, centered on a "Holy Grail War" where participants must kill or be killed. However, it is defined by a sharp contrast between two extremes:
*   **Grim Realism:** The series does not shy away from the brutality of war, featuring graphic violence, psychological trauma, and "Bad Endings" where the protagonist can suffer horrific deaths.
*   **Slice-of-Life Levity:** Between life-or-death battles, the story spends significant time on mundane activities like cooking, school life, and domestic interactions. This serves to humanize the characters and heighten the impact when that peace is shattered.

### **2. Themes: Heroism and Ideals**
The central thematic pillar is the **nature of heroism** and the **conflict between ideals and reality**.
*   **The "Hero of Justice":** Protagonist Shirou Emiya pursues an ideal of saving everyone, a wish he "borrowed" from his foster father. The series explores whether this self-sacrificial path is noble, hypocritical, or a form of mental illness.
*   **Self-Deconstruction:** Each route interrogates Shirou’s ideals differently:
    *   *Fate:* Acceptance of the ideal as a beautiful, albeit impossible, dream.
    *   *Unlimited Blade Works:* Confronting the logical conclusion of the ideal (represented by Archer) and choosing to pursue it despite knowing its futility.
    *   *Heaven’s Feel:* The rejection of the universal ideal in favor of protecting a single loved one.
*   **Fate vs. Choice:** Characters often struggle against predetermined destinies or the "weight" of their historical legends (Heroic Spirits).

### **3. Dark Comedy: The "Tiger Dojo"**
While the main plot is rarely a "comedy," *Fate/stay night* incorporates **dark comedy** primarily through its meta-elements:
*   **Tiger Dojo:** After every "Bad End," players are sent to a comedic, fourth-wall-breaking segment where characters Taiga Fujimura and Ilyasviel von Einzbern mock the player's failure and provide hints. The humor is often "dark" because it immediately follows a tragic or gruesome death.
*   **Absurdist Juxtaposition:** The series finds humor in the absurdity of legendary figures (like King Arthur or Heracles) trying to navigate modern Japanese life, such as Saber’s obsession with modern food or the "Lancer ga Shinda!" (Lancer died!) running gag in spin-offs.

### **4. Optimism: Beauty in the Struggle**
Despite its dark reputation, *Fate/stay night* is fundamentally **optimistic**. It is often described as a story about "the beauty of a dream that cannot be reached."
*   **Perseverance:** The narrative rewards the "will to continue" despite overwhelming odds. Even in its most tragic moments, the story emphasizes that the effort to do good has intrinsic value, regardless of the outcome.
*   **Human Connection:** Optimism is found in the bonds formed between Masters and Servants, suggesting that even "tools of war" can find mutual understanding and redemption.

### **5. Atmosphere and Mood**
*   **Atmosphere:** The series utilizes an **Urban Fantasy** atmosphere, where ancient magic and mythical legends hide beneath the surface of a contemporary Japanese city (Fuyuki). The atmosphere shifts from **mystical and adventurous** in the first route to **oppressive and horrific** in the final route.
*   **Mood:** The mood is often **suspenseful and melancholic**, punctuated by "epic" moments of high-octane action. There is a persistent sense of "impending doom" as the Holy Grail War reaches its climax.

### **6. Genre**
*   **Primary Genre:** Dark Fantasy / Urban Fantasy.
*   **Sub-genres:** Battle Seinen (aimed at young adult men), Mystery, and Psychological Drama.
*   **Format:** Originally a Visual Novel (Eroge), it has been adapted into various anime formats that lean more toward "Battle Shonen" (action-focused for younger audiences) or "Seinen" (mature drama).
