## Characters
*Hellsing Ultimate* is a 10-episode OVA series that follows the Hellsing Organization, a secret British task force dedicated to protecting the Queen and the country from supernatural threats. The story centers on a three-way war between Hellsing, the Vatican’s Iscariot Organization, and Millennium, a rogue Nazi battalion.

### **The Protagonists (Hellsing Organization)**

#### **Alucard (The Trump Card)**
*   **Role:** The primary protagonist and the Hellsing Organization's most powerful weapon. He is the original vampire, Count Dracula, who was defeated by Abraham Van Helsing and bound to serve his descendants.
*   **Abilities:** 
    *   **Immortality & Regeneration:** Virtually unkillable; he can regenerate from being reduced to a puddle of blood.
    *   **Cromwell Invocation:** A system of seals (Levels 3 to 0) that restrict his power. At **Level 0**, he releases all the millions of souls he has consumed, manifesting them as a literal army of the undead.
    *   **Shapeshifting:** Can transform into a giant hound (Baskerville), bats, mist, or various human forms.
    *   **Weaponry:** Wields two oversized handguns, the **.454 Casull** and the **Jackal**, which fire explosive silver bullets.
*   **Relationships:** He shares a deep, complex bond of loyalty and respect with **Integra**. He acts as a mentor and "father figure" to **Seras**. He views **Alexander Anderson** as his only worthy human rival.

#### **Sir Integra Fairbrook Wingates Hellsing**
*   **Role:** The leader of the Hellsing Organization and Alucard’s master. She inherited the position at age 12 after her father’s death.
*   **Abilities:** 
    *   **Indomitable Will:** Her greatest strength is her mental fortitude; she is one of the few humans Alucard truly respects.
    *   **Combat Skill:** An expert fencer and markswoman.
*   **Relationships:** She is the only person Alucard obeys. She is a strict but protective leader to **Seras** and was deeply betrayed by her lifelong butler, **Walter**.

#### **Seras Victoria (The Police Girl)**
*   **Role:** A former police officer turned into a fledgling vampire by Alucard. She serves as the "heart" of the series, struggling to maintain her humanity.
*   **Abilities:** 
    *   **Vampiric Strength:** Wields the **Harkonnen**, a massive 30mm anti-tank cannon.
    *   **Third Eye:** Allows her to see through illusions and fire with perfect accuracy over miles.
    *   **Shadow Manipulation:** After consuming the soul of **Pip Bernadotte**, she becomes a "True Vampire," replacing her severed arm with a shapeshifting shadow wing.
*   **Relationships:** She is Alucard’s "fledgling" and loyal servant. She develops a tragic romantic bond with the mercenary leader **Pip Bernadotte**.

---

### **The Antagonists**

#### **The Major (The Primary Antagonist)**
*   **Role:** The leader of **Millennium**. An SS-Sturmbannführer who survived WWII and spent 50 years creating an army of 1,000 artificial vampires to start an "endless war."
*   **Abilities:** 
    *   **Master Strategist:** He has no supernatural powers and is a terrible shot, but his genius lies in manipulation and grand-scale tactical warfare.
    *   **Cyborg Physiology:** He replaced his aging body with machinery to survive long enough to see his war through.
*   **Relationships:** He is obsessed with Alucard, not to kill him out of hate, but to prove that a human "will" can defeat a "monster."

#### **Alexander Anderson (The Rival)**
*   **Role:** A paladin of the Vatican’s **Iscariot (Section XIII)**. He is a fanatical Catholic who views all vampires—including Alucard—as abominations.
*   **Abilities:** 
    *   **Regenerator:** A bio-engineered human capable of healing from gunshots to the head.
    *   **Blessed Bayonets:** Wields an infinite supply of holy blades that can burn vampires.
    *   **Nail of Helena:** In their final fight, he stabs himself with a holy relic to become the "Monster of God," a plant-like entity of pure holy fire.
*   **Relationships:** He is Alucard's arch-nemesis. Despite their mutual hatred, they share a grim respect for each other's combat prowess.

#### **Walter C. Dornez (The Angel of Death)**
*   **Role:** The Hellsing family butler who later betrays them to join Millennium.
*   **Abilities:** 
    *   **Micro-filament Wires:** Uses razor-sharp, nearly invisible wires to slice through steel, buildings, and armies.
    *   **Artificial Vampirism:** After his betrayal, he is rejuvenated into his younger, more powerful self, though his body becomes unstable.
*   **Relationships:** He was a father figure to Integra and a former partner-in-arms to Alucard. His betrayal stems from a lifelong jealousy of Alucard’s immortality and power.

---

### **Key Supporting Characters**
*   **The Captain:** The Major’s silent bodyguard and a natural **Werewolf**. He is one of the few characters physically capable of matching Alucard and Seras.
*   **Pip Bernadotte:** Leader of the "Wild Geese" mercenary group. He provides tactical support to Hellsing and becomes Seras's familiar after his death.
*   **Schrödinger:** A cat-boy with the ability to be "everywhere and nowhere." He is the key to the Major’s plan to defeat Alucard by poisoning his consciousness.

## Locations
The world of *Hellsing Ultimate* is set in a dark, supernatural version of the late 20th century (specifically 1999). While the story primarily centers on London and the English countryside, it spans several global locations that reflect the influence of the three main factions: the Hellsing Organization, Millennium, and Iscariot.

### **1. The United Kingdom (Hellsing Headquarters)**
The UK serves as the primary battleground and the home of the Royal Order of Protestant Knights.

*   **Hellsing Mansion:** Located in the countryside on the outskirts of **London** (specifically identified in lore as **Finchley**, NW London). It is a massive, fortified estate that serves as the Hellsing Organization's headquarters, housing Alucard’s basement dungeon, state-of-the-art training facilities, and the Round Table’s meeting rooms.
*   **London:** The capital is the site of the series' apocalyptic finale. During the Millennium invasion, many real-world landmarks are featured and subsequently destroyed:
    *   **The Tower of London:** A historic fortress where Alucard often manifests his power.
    *   **Tower Bridge:** Heavily damaged during the Nazi vampire assault.
    *   **Trafalgar Square & St. Paul’s Cathedral:** Key sites of the three-way war between Hellsing, Millennium, and the Vatican’s 9th Crusade.
    *   **HMS Eagle:** A hijacked Royal Navy aircraft carrier that Rip Van Winkle uses as a base before Alucard crashes a jet into it.
*   **Cheddar Village:** The rural village where the series begins. It is the site of the priest-vampire incident where Alucard first meets and turns Seras Victoria.

### **2. South America (Millennium’s Hiding Place)**
After their defeat in World War II, the remnants of the Nazi "Millennium" group fled to South America to rebuild their forces.

*   **Rio de Janeiro, Brazil:** A pivotal location in the middle of the series. Alucard and Seras travel here to investigate Millennium's activities.
*   **The Rio Hotel:** The specific site of the brutal battle between Alucard and **Tubalcain Alhambra** (The Dandy Man). The hotel is besieged by a corrupt Brazilian SWAT team (GATE) manipulated by Millennium, leading to a bloodbath in the hotel’s hallways and rooftop.

### **3. Vatican City (Iscariot Headquarters)**
The sovereign city-state within Rome serves as the base for **Section XIII Iscariot**, the Vatican's secret branch for exterminating heretics and monsters.

*   **The Vatican:** Featured in scenes involving Enrico Maxwell and the Pope. It is depicted as a place of immense political and religious power, housing the "Iscariot" archives and the staging ground for the **9th Crusade**, a fanatical army sent to "purify" London.

### **4. Historical Locations (Hellsing: The Dawn)**
The prequel series, *The Dawn*, provides geographical context for the origins of the conflict.

*   **Warsaw, Poland (1944):** The location of Millennium’s original research facility during WWII. A young Walter C. Dornez and Alucard (in the form of a young girl) are sent here to destroy the Nazi vampire project, which they mistakenly believe they succeeded in doing.

### **5. The "Moving" Locations**
Several of the most important "places" in the series are actually mobile military assets.

*   **The Hindenburg II:** Millennium’s massive flagship Zeppelin. It serves as the Major’s command center and the site of the final confrontation between Integra, Seras, and the Millennium leadership.
*   **The Letztes Bataillon (Last Battalion):** While not a physical place, this force of 1,000 SS vampires functions as a mobile "nation" of sorts, moving from their hidden South American bases to the skies over London.

## Power System
The power system in *Hellsing Ultimate* is a dark, occult-based hierarchy where the soul is the ultimate currency. It blends traditional vampire folklore with modern alchemy, genetic engineering, and divine intervention.

### **1. Core Mechanics: The Soul & Blood**
The fundamental rule of the *Hellsing* universe is that **"Blood is the coinage of the soul."**
*   **Soul Absorption:** When a vampire drinks the blood of a human, they do not just take their life; they take their entire existence—their memories, skills, and soul. 
*   **Extra Lives:** Each soul absorbed acts as a "stock" or an extra life. If Alucard is "killed," he simply sacrifices one of the millions of souls inside him to regenerate instantly. This makes him virtually unkillable as long as he has a reserve of souls.
*   **Familiar Summoning:** A vampire can manifest the souls they have consumed as "familiars"—servants who fight on their behalf. These familiars retain the abilities they had in life (e.g., Rip Van Winkle’s magic bullets or Tubalcain Alhambra’s cards).

### **2. Supernatural Beings & Abilities**
The power system is divided into several distinct categories of beings:

| Category | Description | Key Abilities |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **True Vampires** | Natural-born or sired by a True Vampire (e.g., Alucard, Seras). | High-tier regeneration, shapeshifting, telekinesis, and soul-stocking. |
| **Fledglings** | A vampire who has been sired but has not yet consumed blood to become "True." | Enhanced strength/speed but limited regeneration and no soul-stocking. |
| **Artificial Vampires** | Created via "Freak" chips using the DNA of Mina Harker (Millennium). | Fixed power levels; they are often stronger than weak natural vampires but cannot evolve or stock souls. |
| **Ghouls** | Humans drained of blood who were not virgins. | Mindless, zombie-like thralls controlled by the vampire who turned them. |
| **Regenerators** | Humans enhanced by Iscariot (Vatican) through genetic engineering. | Healing factors that rival vampires, allowing them to survive lethal wounds. |
| **Werewolves** | Natural supernatural beings (e.g., The Captain). | Immense physical durability and strength; immune to most vampire-killing methods except silver. |

### **3. Rules & Limitations**
*   **The Virginity Rule:** To become a vampire, a human must be a virgin of the opposite sex when bitten. If they are not, they become a mindless Ghoul.
*   **Native Soil:** Traditional vampires must sleep in a coffin filled with the soil of their homeland to maintain their strength.
*   **Weaknesses:** 
    *   **Silver & Holy Weapons:** Silver slows regeneration, and blessed weapons (like Anderson’s bayonets) can cause permanent damage to a vampire's soul-stock.
    *   **Sunlight:** Most vampires are weakened by it, though high-tier vampires like Alucard can resist it (albeit with reduced power).
    *   **Invitation:** Vampires cannot enter a private home without being invited.

### **4. Power Tiers: The Cromwell Invocation**
Alucard’s power is so immense that the Hellsing Organization placed alchemical seals on him to restrict his abilities. These are known as the **Control Art Restriction System**.

*   **Levels 6 to 3:** Standard combat levels. Alucard uses guns (the Casull and Jackal) and basic supernatural speed.
*   **Level 2 & 1:** Released for high-threat targets (e.g., Luke Valentine). Alucard becomes a mass of shifting shadows and eyes, often summoning the **Hellhound (Baskerville)**.
*   **Level 0:** The ultimate release. Alucard manifests every soul he has ever consumed (over 3 million) as an undead army. 
    *   *Trade-off:* While Level 0 is his most destructive state, it is also his most vulnerable. Because all his "lives" are outside his body, a single strike to his heart in this form can kill him permanently.

### **5. Progression & Evolution**
*   **Seras Victoria:** Progresses from a "Fledgling" (relying on human weapons like the Harkonnen) to a "True Vampire" after drinking Pip Bernadotte’s blood. This grants her a "Shadow Arm" and the ability to manifest Pip as a familiar.
*   **Alexander Anderson:** Progresses from a "Regenerator" to the **"Monster of God"** by stabbing himself with Helena’s Nail. This elevates him to a divine tier where his thorns can burn away Alucard’s souls, but it costs him his humanity.
*   **Alucard (End of Series):** After absorbing Schrödinger, Alucard gains **Quantum Immortality**. He exists "everywhere and nowhere." As long as he can recognize his own existence, he cannot be killed, erased, or physically contained, effectively making him a god-like entity.

## Factions
In the world of *Hellsing Ultimate*, power is divided between three primary supernatural and political factions, each representing a different religious or ideological pillar. Their conflict culminates in a three-sided apocalyptic war in London.

### 1. The Hellsing Organization
**Full Name:** The Royal Order of Protestant Knights
*   **Leadership:** Sir Integra Fairbrook Wingates Hellsing
*   **Headquarters:** Hellsing Manor, outskirts of London, England
*   **Goal:** To protect the British Empire and the Anglican Church from supernatural threats (vampires, ghouls, etc.) with "extreme prejudice."

**Key Members & Assets:**
*   **Alucard:** The "Trump Card" of Hellsing. He is the original vampire (Dracula), bound to the Hellsing family by Sir Abraham Van Helsing a century prior. He possesses near-omnipotent regenerative abilities and a massive arsenal of familiars.
*   **Seras Victoria:** A former police officer turned into a fledgling vampire by Alucard. She eventually becomes a "True Vampire" and a powerhouse in her own right.
*   **Walter C. Dornez:** The family butler and former "Angel of Death." He uses razor-sharp micro-filament wires as weapons.
*   **The Wild Geese:** A professional mercenary group led by **Pip Bernadotte**, hired to replace Hellsing’s regular soldiers after a devastating internal attack.

**World Power Influence:**
Hellsing is governed and funded by the **Round Table Council** (also known as the Convention of Twelve), a secret society of British aristocrats and high-ranking military officials who rule England from the shadows.

---

### 2. Millennium
**Full Name:** The Letztes Bataillon (The Last Battalion)
*   **Leadership:** The Major (a former SS officer)
*   **Headquarters:** The *Deus Ex Machina* (a fleet of massive zeppelins)
*   **Goal:** To wage an "eternal war" and destroy Alucard. They represent the remnants of Nazi Germany who fled to South America after WWII.

**Key Units & Members:**
*   **The Werwolf:** An elite special forces unit of supernatural beings.
    *   **The Captain:** A natural werewolf and the Major's silent bodyguard.
    *   **Schrödinger:** A "werecat" with the ability to be "everywhere and nowhere," making him effectively immortal.
    *   **Rip van Winkle & Zorin Blitz:** Powerful lieutenants with unique abilities (magic bullets and illusions, respectively).
*   **The Last Battalion:** An army of 1,000 artificial vampires created using the remains of Mina Harker (who still carried Alucard's essence).
*   **The Doctor:** The mad scientist responsible for the vampire-creation process.

**World Power Influence:**
Millennium spent 50 years in hiding in Brazil, utilizing "Ratlines" (Vatican-assisted escape routes) to evade capture after WWII while building their undead army.

---

### 3. Iscariot (Vatican Section XIII)
**Full Name:** The Iscariot Organization
*   **Leadership:** Archbishop Enrico Maxwell
*   **Headquarters:** Vatican City
*   **Goal:** To exterminate all "unholy" beings, including vampires and "heretics" (specifically Protestants). They are the Catholic Church’s secret weapon.

**Key Members & Assets:**
*   **Alexander Anderson:** A "Regenerator" and Paladin who serves as Alucard’s primary rival. He uses blessed bayonets and holy barriers.
*   **Heinkel Wolfe & Yumie Takagi:** An assassin duo (a pistol-wielding agent and a katana-wielding nun).
*   **The Ninth Crusade:** A massive military force of 3,000+ fanatical knights from various orders (Malta, Santo Stefano, etc.) launched by Maxwell to "purify" London of both Nazis and Protestants.

**World Power Influence:**
As a secret wing of the Vatican, Iscariot wields immense religious and political influence. During the London invasion, Maxwell is promoted to Archbishop, granting him command over the Vatican's entire military might.

## Combat
Combat in *Hellsing Ultimate* is defined by a "rule of cool" philosophy that blends hyper-violent spectacle with deep psychological and tactical layers. The series uses its battles not just for action, but as the primary vehicle for character development and thematic exploration.

### **1. Core Fighting Styles**
Each major combatant utilizes a distinct, often supernatural, fighting style that reflects their personality and history.

*   **Alucard (The Ultimate Predator):**
    *   **Style:** "Understate, Outlast, Overpower." Alucard typically begins a fight by allowing his opponent to "kill" him, absorbing their best attacks to gauge their worth. Once he deems them unworthy or has had his fun, he reveals overwhelming power.
    *   **Arsenal:** He uses two custom-made oversized handguns—the **.454 Casull** and the **Jackal**—which fire explosive silver-cross-tipped bullets.
    *   **Abilities:** Beyond marksmanship, he uses shapeshifting (turning into a mass of eyes and shadows or a giant hound), intangibility, and the **Cromwell Invocation**, which releases seals on his power. At **Level 0**, he summons the millions of souls he has consumed as a literal army of the undead.
*   **Seras Victoria (The Evolving Soldier):**
    *   **Style:** Heavy Ordnance and Shadow Manipulation. Initially a hesitant police officer, she relies on the **Harkonnen** anti-Midian cannon.
    *   **True Vampire Form:** After her "ascension," her style shifts. She replaces her severed arm with a wing of solid shadow and blood, using it as a whip, a shield, or a swarm of spikes. Her combat becomes more feral and instinctive, driven by the soul of Pip Bernadotte residing within her.
*   **Alexander Anderson (The Holy Monster):**
    *   **Style:** Relentless Zealotry. He uses an infinite supply of **blessed bayonets** for both melee and throwing. He utilizes **Bible pages** to create barriers and nullify vampire magic.
    *   **Monster of God:** By impaling himself with the **Nail of Helena**, he transforms into a plant-like entity of thorns and holy fire, sacrificing his humanity to become a "miracle" capable of killing Alucard.
*   **Walter C. Dornez (The Angel of Death):**
    *   **Style:** Precision and Control. Walter manipulates nearly invisible **monofilament wires** with surgical precision, capable of slicing through buildings, blocking bullets, and controlling enemies like puppets.
*   **The Captain (The Silent Beast):**
    *   **Style:** Stoic Brutality. A natural werewolf who uses hand-to-hand combat and a mist-like intangibility to bypass defenses. He is arguably the most durable character, possessing skin that can stop Walter’s wires bare-handed.

### **2. Tactical and Strategic Depth**
Despite the flashy powers, battles are often won through long-term planning and psychological warfare.

*   **The Major’s Grand Strategy:** The entire London invasion was a tactical trap designed to kill Alucard. The Major sacrificed his own army (Millennium) and manipulated the Vatican (Iscariot) to force Alucard into releasing **Level 0**. This left Alucard vulnerable to a final "poisoning" of his soul via Schrödinger’s paradox.
*   **The Wild Geese Defense:** During the siege of Hellsing Manor, the human mercenaries used realistic military tactics—claymores, mines, and choke points—to hold off superior vampire forces. This highlighted the "human vs. monster" theme, showing that even without powers, strategy can bridge the gap.
*   **Iscariot’s Escalation:** The Vatican’s Section XIII uses "scorched earth" tactics, viewing both the Nazi vampires and the Protestant Hellsing organization as heretics to be purged simultaneously, regardless of civilian collateral.

### **3. Emotional Stakes**
Battles in *Hellsing Ultimate* are rarely just about survival; they are about the clash of ideologies.

*   **The Tragedy of Immortality:** Alucard’s fights are tinged with a deep sadness. He despises monsters who throw away their humanity and deeply respects humans (like Anderson or Integra) who have the willpower to stand against him. His final duel with Anderson is a moment of mutual respect and mourning for the "human" Anderson lost.
*   **Seras and Pip:** The battle against Zorin Blitz is the emotional heart of the series. Seras’s transformation is triggered by the death of Pip Bernadotte, turning a scene of trauma and defeat into a triumphant, albeit bloody, rebirth.
*   **Walter’s Obsession:** Walter’s betrayal is driven by the fear of becoming "obsolete." His fight with Alucard is a desperate, pathetic attempt to reclaim his youth and prove he is the superior "Angel of Death," only to realize he traded his soul for a flawed, temporary prime.

### **4. Flashy Spectacle and Visual Style**
The series is famous for its high-budget animation (produced by Satlight, Madhouse, and Graphinica) and its "oppressive" visual style.

*   **Cinematography:** The use of extreme close-ups on eyes, wide shots of blood-soaked London, and the "ink-blot" style of Alucard’s shadows creates a gothic, nightmarish atmosphere.
*   **Iconic Moments:**
    *   **The London Invasion:** A massive-scale spectacle of zeppelins, V1 rockets, and thousands of vampires descending on the city.
    *   **Level 0 Release:** The visual of a literal sea of blood and corpses flooding the streets of London is one of the most famous "spectacle" moments in anime history.
    *   **The Rule of Cool:** Logic often takes a backseat to badassery—such as Alucard catching a magic card in his teeth or Seras using a 345kg cannon like a sniper rifle.

## Tone
*Hellsing Ultimate* is a 10-episode OVA series renowned for its "grand opera" approach to supernatural violence. It is a faithful adaptation of Kouta Hirano’s manga, characterized by an unapologetic "Rule of Cool" philosophy that balances high-stakes horror with absurd, stylized action.

### **1. Tone and Mood**
The tone of *Hellsing Ultimate* is **theatrical, over-the-top, and hyper-violent.** It is often described as "going up to 11," where every battle is treated as a monumental event.
*   **Mood:** The mood shifts between **supernatural menace** and **adrenaline-fueled "badassery."** While the series deals with grim subjects like genocide and the loss of humanity, it rarely feels nihilistic because the protagonists embrace their roles with a charismatic, almost joyful ferocity.
*   **Stylization:** The show uses "heavy" visual direction—deep shadows, high-contrast colors (especially crimson), and dramatic "final boss" framing for its characters.

### **2. Themes**
*   **Humanity vs. Monstrosity:** A central question is what it means to be "human." Alucard, the protagonist, expresses a deep-seated respect for humans who refuse to give up their mortality, while viewing those who seek immortality (like the Nazi vampires) as pathetic cowards.
*   **The Nature of Evil:** The series explores a "three-way war" between different versions of fanaticism: the Protestant Hellsing Organization, the Catholic Iscariot Organization, and the Nazi Millennium group. It suggests that "good" and "evil" are often just different shades of bloodlust.
*   **Legacy and Old Threats:** The theme that "old threats never truly die" is literalized by the return of a hidden Nazi battalion from WWII, forcing the characters to confront the ghosts of history.

### **3. Dark Comedy**
*Hellsing Ultimate* is famous (and sometimes criticized) for its jarring shifts into **slapstick and "chibi" humor.**
*   **Visual Gags:** Serious characters like Seras Victoria or even Alucard will suddenly be drawn in a simplified, "chibi" art style to perform comedic skits. These moments usually involve Seras being a "ditz" or Alucard annoying his master, Integra.
*   **Absurdity:** The dark comedy often stems from the sheer absurdity of the situations—such as a vampire using a 30mm anti-tank cannon as a standard sidearm or the Vatican launching a literal crusade on modern-day London.

### **4. Optimism**
Despite the "edge" and gore, there is a surprising vein of **optimism** found in the character of **Seras Victoria.**
*   **The Moral Compass:** Seras represents the "sliver of humanity" that remains. Her arc is one of growth; she transitions from a terrified victim to a powerful warrior without losing her kindness or soul.
*   **The Ending:** While the series concludes with the near-total destruction of London, the final scenes (set 30 years later) show a resilient Hellsing Organization. The survival of the bond between Integra, Seras, and a returned Alucard provides a "hopeful" resolution to an otherwise apocalyptic story.

### **5. Atmosphere and Genre**
*   **Genre:** It sits firmly in **Seinen (adult) Action, Supernatural Horror, and Dark Fantasy.**
*   **Atmosphere:** The atmosphere is **Gothic and Dystopian.** It feels like a "Wagnerian Opera" set in a world of modern warfare.
*   **Music:** The score, performed by the **Warsaw Philharmonic Orchestra**, mixes grand orchestral arrangements with heavy rock and jazz, reinforcing the clash between ancient vampiric lore and modern military technology.

| Element | Description |

| **Primary Tone** | Grandiose, theatrical, and hyper-violent. |
| **Dark Comedy** | Polarizing "chibi" skits and absurd, over-the-top weaponry. |
| **Optimism** | Found in Seras Victoria's refusal to lose her humanity. |
| **Atmosphere** | Gothic grandeur meets modern military carnage. |
| **Mood** | Intense, "badass," and occasionally spooky. |

## Arcs, Sequels, Adaptations, Recent
*Hellsing Ultimate* (2006–2012) is a 10-episode Original Video Animation (OVA) series that serves as a faithful adaptation of Kouta Hirano’s original *Hellsing* manga. It was created to replace the 2001 TV series, which diverged significantly from the source material.

### **1. Major Story Arcs & Plot Progression**
The story is structured into several key arcs, often collectively referred to as the **"London War"** from Volume 5 onwards.

*   **Vampire Hunter Arc (Episodes 1–2):** 
    *   **Events:** Introduction of the Hellsing Organization and its trump card, the vampire **Alucard**. In Cheddar Village, Alucard turns police officer **Seras Victoria** into a fledgling vampire to save her life. 
    *   **Key Conflict:** Alucard’s first encounter with **Alexander Anderson**, a paladin from the Vatican’s Section XIII (Iscariot).
*   **Dead Zone / Valentine Brothers Arc (Episode 3):** 
    *   **Events:** The Hellsing headquarters is attacked by the **Valentine Brothers** (Jan and Luke) and an army of ghouls. 
    *   **Key Conflict:** This reveals the existence of "artificial vampires" and the first mention of **Millennium**, a Nazi remnant group.
*   **Rio de Janeiro Arc (Episode 4):** 
    *   **Events:** Alucard and Seras travel to Brazil to investigate Millennium. They are branded as terrorists by the corrupt local police (manipulated by Millennium).
    *   **Key Conflict:** Alucard fights **Tubalcain Alhambra** and receives a direct challenge from **The Major**, the leader of Millennium.
*   **London Invasion / The Great War (Episodes 5–9):** 
    *   **Events:** Millennium launches a full-scale assault on London with a battalion of 1,000 SS vampires. Simultaneously, the Vatican (Iscariot) launches a "crusade" to purge both the Nazis and the Protestant Hellsing Organization.
    *   **Key Conflict:** The three-way war peaks with Alucard releasing "Level Zero," summoning all the souls he has ever consumed. This leads to the final showdown with Alexander Anderson and the betrayal of **Walter C. Dornez**, who joins Millennium to regain his youth and fight Alucard.
*   **Final Battle / Black Onyx Arc (Episode 10):** 
    *   **Events:** Alucard is "erased" from existence by **Schrödinger**, who allows himself to be consumed by Alucard, causing Alucard’s consciousness to vanish. Integra and Seras finish off The Major.
    *   **Timeline Jump:** The series ends with a 30-year time skip, showing an aged Integra and the eventual return of Alucard.

### **2. Sequels, Spinoffs, and Continuity**
*   **Hellsing: The Dawn (Prequel):** 
    *   Set in 1944, it follows a 14-year-old Walter and Alucard (appearing as a young girl) as they attack Millennium’s base in Warsaw during WWII. 
    *   **Status:** The manga is incomplete (6 chapters). Three short animated episodes were released as specials with *Hellsing Ultimate* Volumes VIII, IX, and X.
*   **Hellsing (2001 TV Series):** 
    *   An earlier adaptation by Studio Gonzo. It follows the manga for the first few episodes but then creates an entirely original story involving a villain named **Incognito**. It is considered **non-canon** to the main manga timeline.
*   **Crossfire (Side Story):** 
    *   A side-manga focusing on Iscariot agents **Heinkel Wolfe** and **Yumie Takagi**. While it exists in the same universe, it was never fully integrated into the *Ultimate* anime.

### **3. Manga vs. Anime: Differences & Adaptations**
*   **Fidelity:** *Hellsing Ultimate* is almost 100% faithful to the manga’s plot, whereas the 2001 anime is roughly 20% faithful.
*   **Visual Style:** *Ultimate* uses a high-budget OVA format, allowing for extreme gore and fluid animation that matches Hirano’s "ink-heavy" and gritty art style. The 2001 series has a more "noir" and atmospheric 90s aesthetic.
*   **Characterization:** In the 2001 anime, Seras is the main protagonist/audience surrogate. In *Ultimate* and the manga, the focus shifts more toward Alucard and the philosophical rivalry between him, Anderson, and The Major.
*   **The Major:** One of the biggest differences is the presence of **The Major**. He is the primary antagonist of the manga and *Ultimate* but is entirely absent from the 2001 series.

### **4. Latest News & Current Events (2024–2025)**
The *Hellsing* story is officially **complete**, with no new chapters or episodes currently in production. However, the franchise remains active through other media:

*   **Live-Action Movie (In Development):** Amazon Studios is developing a live-action *Hellsing* feature film. **Derek Kolstad** (creator/writer of *John Wick*) is attached to write and produce. As of late 2024, the script is reportedly finalized, though casting has not been officially announced.
*   **Kouta Hirano Exhibition (2025):** A major exhibition for creator Kouta Hirano is scheduled for **March 27 – April 13, 2025**, in Tokyo. It will feature over 200 original drawings from both *Hellsing* and his other major work, *Drifters*.
*   **Streaming & Re-releases:** The series was recently moved to **Crunchyroll** following the Funimation merger, keeping it accessible to modern audiences. There are occasional rumors of a "revival" or a 4K remaster, but nothing has been confirmed by the publisher, Shonen Gahosha.
