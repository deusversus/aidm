# M2R2 — The exit: killing the deflating close

**Trigger (2026-07-20):** the player reports narrations answer their action but never drive to the next action point — "the story refuses to move forward unless I spell out exactly what happens next." A three-agent investigation (all 12 real played turns classified; v3 reference archaeology; full prompt-machinery map) confirmed and sharpened it.

## The evidence

- **World initiative is fine** (12/12 turns had NPCs acting unprompted — C8 works). The failure is at the **close**: pressure introduced mid-scene is *defused before the curtain* ("he forgot it. Mostly." / "the flat brown water going quiet again" / "without, yet, thinking anything at all"). The KA's prose instinct — end on a resolved cadence — actively kills its own hooks.
- **Altitude-mirroring:** detailed player inputs drew the REST endings; bare "continue" produced the most plot-forward turn in the dataset. The engine treats rich input as the ceiling instead of the floor.
- **The fork treadmill:** one campaign ended 5/6 turns on a fork — the *same standing dilemma re-posed*, not new motion.
- **The machinery codifies rest:** the contract's only exit instruction is "present it and STOP"; the sidecar blesses "presented and stopped"; the Settei tells centered campaigns "moderate, unforced"; the only "plant one seed of what comes next / end on an image" text lives in sakuga fragments (rare scenes) and the opening-scene path (which is why both campaigns' T1s ended on MOTION and nothing after did).

## Ledger entry (§11/§14 risk 6 discipline)

**Named failure mode: the deflating close** — the engine introduces pressure and neutralizes it in the same scene; the story idles unless the player scripts the next event, and even then the engine mirrors instead of compounding.
**Pillar:** §0 (stories that move you require stories that move) + §7 stakes doctrine + §5.1's decision-point stop rule, which was shipped without its complement.
**Status of each piece:** items 2–3 below are dropped-v3-mechanism / defect repairs (no new ledger surface); item 1 generalizes an instruction v3 carried only in its opening-scene prompt — this entry is its ledger row; item 4 renders contract fields that already exist; item 5 is measurement (axiom: measured, not vibed).

## Deliverables (one commit)

1. **"The exit" — a KA-contract faculty** (ka.ts, between The camera and the trailer close, which stays LAST — the pinned recency test governs). The law: pressure introduced in a scene survives it unless deliberately paid off; end on the thing that asks (arrival, discovery landing, presented fork, advancing clock), never on its deflation; the player's input is the floor, not the ceiling (render their half, then make the world's move); new or advanced pressure, never the same fork re-worn; rest is a register the charter licenses, not a per-scene default — and even a rest scene leaves one live thread visibly open.
2. **Sacred Rule #3 restored** (dropped v3 mechanism, vibe_keeper.md:17-19): a "THE WORLD MOVES" non-negotiable paired directly with the agency bullet — NPCs have goals, secrets, and reactions that are not responses to the player; they act between scenes and within them. Never decide FOR the player; always leave them something to decide ABOUT.
3. **`pacing_note` wired** (defect — computed every turn at pacer.ts:304, never written into the conte): PacerBeat gains `pacing_note`; layout passes it; renderConte's Beat section renders it. The Pacer's system text upgrades the note's duty: when the beat wants a driving close, the note NAMES the pressure the scene should end on (per-turn variety so the standing faculty doesn't yield same-shaped hooks), and never re-poses the prior turns' standing fork (recentBeats is already in its prompt).
4. **The premise governor renders** (contract fields with no reader): `escalation_pattern` + `story_time_density` render as one "How it moves" line in the Settei identity block — Block-1 placement because they are standing premise facts, sparing the scene-shape ≤150 budget. Cost: ~40 charter tokens; on maxed charters the trim ladder may drop a third exemplar — accepted trade, surfaced here. The stable/mushishi register line is what licenses rest endings (the user's "some campaigns prefer slow").
5. **The exit gauge** (scripts/exit-gauge.ts, soak-lib pattern): on-demand classification of a campaign's last N turn endings — MOTION / FORK / REST, pressure-alive-at-close, treadmill detection — via dev-tier probes (never Fable), reporting percentages. The investigation's manual rubric, mechanized, so the fix is measured and can't silently regress.

6. **Coherent token budgets** (user-directed 2026-07-20: "make sure responses have AMPLE room to breathe, and that brevity or bombasticism is controlled by the campaign's needs rather than happenstantially set limits on the plumbing"). The inventory found the KA's thinking pad (+24k) and truncation-throw to be LOCAL hardening while ~25 other call sites run adaptive thinking inside flat un-padded caps with no truncation detection (a clip reads as a parse failure and retries into the same jar); recap/yokoku/booth prose sit at 4000 as accidental style governors; three calls ride an invisible 1024 default; entity_merge_pair caps at 400. The scheme:
   - **Structural thinking headroom at the choke point** (calls.ts): effective max_tokens = declared output budget + pad(model, effort) — pad 0 on non-thinking models, effort-scaled on adaptive-thinking models (low 8k / medium 12k / high 16k / xhigh+ 32k; no-effort calls pad 8k) — clamped to the model's real max output (MODEL_CAPS grows a maxOutput column if absent). The KA's local +24_000 is removed, absorbed by the mechanism.
   - **Loud truncation everywhere**: stop_reason max_tokens → console.warn with call name + budgets + trace tag, in both callStructured and the narration path; the structured corrective retry doubles the OUTPUT budget once (clamped) when truncation caused the failure; collectNarration/booth responder/SZ conductor inspect stopReason and warn instead of returning clipped prose as complete.
   - **One budgets module** (src/lib/llm/budgets.ts): named output-budget classes with rationale — CLASSIFY 1_000 (router, transition check, merge pair), STRUCTURED_SMALL 2_000 (triage, pacer, validation, relevance, extraction, revision, sidecar fallback, suggestions), STRUCTURED_RICH 8_000 (outcome, scale, sakkan, compaction, distill, booth resolution, memo, journal, research calls at their current sizes mapped nearest), LOOPED_LARGE 16_000 (director cycle/startup, OSP synthesis), PROSE_COMPOSER 8_000 (recap, yokoku, booth responder, SZ conductor). Call sites import the class constant; no bare literals, no silent defaults. The KA's per-tier outputBudgetTokens (douga/genga/sakuga) remain the craft-governed component — the ONLY thing that shapes narration length is the premise and the beat; ceilings are safety rails priced only when used.

   **Audit corrections (2026-07-20, folded in before landing):** the `high` thinking pad is 24k, not 16k — genga (the default tier) ran under the old flat +24k and a 16k pad silently shrank its ceiling below measured deep-scene thinking; the Phase-B narration retry doubles the output budget when the prior attempt truncated (retry-same-dice keeps the dice, never the jar — the structured layer's law, mirrored); the Drive line renders the RAW model note only (the joined pacingNote carries strength bookkeeping, which stays engine-side).
